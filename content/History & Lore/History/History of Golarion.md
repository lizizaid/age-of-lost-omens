The history of the world of [[The World of Golarion|Golarion]] is commonly divided into eight ages. The year-counting method of the Inner Sea Region (where our stories take place) is the Absalom Calendar, with Absalom Reckoning (AR), the year in which the god Aroden raised the city of Absalom from the depths, being the first year of the calendar.

## Age of Creation

_(Date: Prehistory, Unknown amount of time)_

The Age of Creation is a term used to describe the uncounted years of mythic time, before civilization itself began. It was at the dawn of this age that the planet of Golarion, the third planet in its solar system, was created. Legends tell that the gods found Golarion to be a perfect world for their creations – thus, they named it The Child, as it was to be a cradle that shall shelter new life.  
As the years progressed, mortal souls began inhabiting this world. However, not long after, a devastating threat from beyond the stars appeared. Rovagug, the Rough Beast, a god of destruction that has consumed countless worlds in his path, set his eyes on Golarion. To protect this young world from devastation, an unlikely alliance of gods came together to defeat Rovagug once and for all. Calistria lured Rovagug to Golarion and distracted him while Torag and Gorum forged the shell of the Dead Vault and Pharasma imbued it with potent wards against escape. Sarenrae then sliced open a rift on Golarion’s surface, sending Rovagug stumbling into the Dead Vault. Zon-Kuthon impaled him with the Star Towers that prevented him from unleashing his power, and the archdevil Asmodeus bound him with a key crafted by Abadar that only the Prince of Darkness could turn. Sarenrae then repaired the rift, leaving behind a smooth scar. Thus, the world itself became Rovagug’s prison for all eternity – a fact that causes astronomers on other worlds to refer to Golarion as The Cage.

## Age of Serpents

_(Date: Prehistory, Unknown amount of time)_

After the battle with Rovagug, a time of peace and flourishment came to Golarion. Humans started building villages and cities. The dwarves built their underground empires, protected by the earth from the outside world. Elves began to appear on Golarion, migrating from the nearby planet of Castrovel by way of portals known as Elf Gates, and populations of Gnomes came to the world as well from the plane of the Fey.  
However, soon the greatest civilization to dominate Golarion was to become the empire of the Serpentfolk, cold blooded snake-like people with cruel intent for the young races of the world. They are said to have ruled portions of the now-destroyed continent of Azlant, as well as most of northern Garund, and parts of Avistan. Serpentfolk subjugated any “lesser” race they encountered, and serpentfolk ruins depict cave-dwelling humans used as cattle or beasts of burden; it is unknown how many races the serpentfolk exterminated for pure pleasure. It is unknown how long Golarion was dominated by the serpentfolk, but even the most conservative estimations put the length of their empire in millennia.  
To the serpentfolk’s surprise, however, a great human nation managed to grow, and in time, outgrow the seprentfolk’s, in their empire’s shadow. The Azlanti human empire, which was created on the now ruined continent of Azlant, rose to prominence, their great and shining cities a beacon to all civilizations of the world. Their warriors knew no equals, and their mages and inventors unlocked arcane secrets unbenkown even to today’s greatest scholars. The serpentfolk civilization was eventually destroyed in a war with the growing Azlanti empire. In a final battle, the serpent god Ydersius was decapitated by Savith, an Azlanti hero; the serpentfolk continued to resist the ascendant Azlanti, but the war was clearly lost, and they eventually abandoned their surface holdings to retreat to the Darklands.

## Age of Legend

_(Date: Pre-Earthfall, Unknown amount of time)_

An age of prosperity and legendary deeds, the Age of Legends saw the Azlanti empire quickly rise in power to become the greatest civilization of the Inner Sea at the time, and perhaps of all times. Other nations came to be from the Azlanti’s people aspirations – to the north, benevolent Emperor Xin created the Thassilonian Empire, based on the study of rune magic but eventually transformed into tyrannus states by his subordinates, the Runelords; and to the east, men found the legendary Dragon Empires in the continent of Tian Xia, where dragons ruled the lands. The empire of Koloran was also founded at that time, ruled by Cyclops, and quickly became the major enemy of the Azlanti people. Soon, however, troubles bigger than Cyclop slavers would plague Azlant.

## Age of Darkness

_(Date: -5293 AR to -4294 AR)_

At the beginning of this age, a great calamity hit the great nations of the past. A great meteor, known as the Starstone, came crashing down from the stars into Golarion, falling onto the continent of Azlant. The result was catastrophical – many empires, including Azlant, Thassilon and Koloran were wiped from the face of the planet, and the continent of Azlant was completely shattered. The dust and ash rising from the impact covered the world’s atmosphere for the next thousand years, blocking sunlight from reaching the people below, thus bringing about an age of darkness. In that darkness, savage monsters and people such as Orcs and Undead thrived, haunting a populace living in a harsh and cold dark world. The impact event would become known as Earthfall, perhaps the most Traumatic event in the history of Golarion.

## Age of Anguish

_(Date: −4294 AR to −3470 AR)_

After a thousand years of darkness, the skies finally began clearing from Earthfall’s dust. However, it took a long time for civilization to get itself back together. This age was characterized by the conflicts of primitive peoples in a desperate struggle for survival and domination in a world left shattered by the Age of Darkness. By its end, some nations managed to overcome their trials and tribulations and leave their mark on history, such as the desert kingdom of Osirion and the Jistka Imperium.

## Age of Destiny

_(Date: −3470 AR to 1 AR)_

Marked by the re-emergence of human civilization after the barbarism of the Age of Darkness and the Age of Anguish, this three and a half millennia time period clearly shows the power of the human spirit. Here, great nations rose once again to fill the world with the light of civilization, including the empire of Taldor and the monarchies of the Lands of the Linnorm Kings. The greatest feat of humankind, however, shall come at the end of that age and start a new age for humanity.

## Age of Enthronement

_(Date: 1 AR to 4606 AR)_

The roots of the Age of Enthronement actually begin in the ancient Azlanti empire, with a man named Aroden, Azlant’s best blacksmith and premier sword maker. Legends say that he wielded many of his powerful swords to battle, protecting the empire from chaos in its final years. As the Starstone fell from the sky, the legends tell, the last emperor of Azlant bestowed upon Aroden his crown, Azlant’s Diamond, and asked him to save their people. The Diamond granted Aroden immortality, and he led the survivors of Earthfall to the east, determined to restore the greatness of the human nation.  
For thousands of years Aroden and his followers achieved great feats, including leading an invasion of the Abyss against a Demon Lord. Aroden traveled the world and the greater universe itself, helping humanity thrive wherever he could. In the year now known as 1 AR, Aroden traveled back to the ruins of Azlant. Using a great ritual, he raised the island of Kortos from the depths of the ocean, and with it, the magical core of the Starstone meteor. By touching the Starstone, Aroden ascended and became a god. He created the city of Absalom on the Isle of Kortos, a shining metropolis and a model for all civilizations across the world. His followers populated the city and constructed the Starstone Cathedral to shelter the artifact. Aroden himself became the god of humanity, history, innovation and destiny, a great hero to many around the world.  
Many years have passed with Absalom as the city in the center of the world. Other mortals have gone there to try the Test of the Starstone, although only three apart from Aroden ever managed to pass it and become gods themselves: Norgorber in 1983, Cayden Cailean in 2765 and Iomedae in 3832. On the mainland, the nations of Andoran and Cheliax were founded as an expansion from the kingdom of Taldor. The Oath Wars raged across the northwest Garund between 2498 AR and 2560 as the churches of Nethys, Norgorber and Sarenrae vied for dominance. The Whispering Tyrant arose in 3203 AR invoking the Shining Crusade in 3754 AR which burned for three quarters of a century and ended with the founding of Lastwall to defend against the return of the lich king. In 4305 AR Cheliax began an extended period of expansion, annexing lands in Molthune and Varisia.

## Age of Lost Omens

_(Date: 4606 AR to Present Day)_

As the nations of the Inner Sea grew, and their people went on with their lives, Aroden has decided to depart the world for now, setting his sights on greater achievements across the Great Beyond. However, he gave his followers a prophecy that he shall return on the cusp of mankind’s greatest triumph and usher in the Age of Glory. Diviners and astrologers managed to calculate the supposed exact time of this event and prepared for it with lavish pomp. On the prophesied date, the entire world waited breathless. But instead of Aroden’s return, all contact with the god was lost, and the world was devastated by terrible storms, the opening of vast planar rifts, and decades-spanning political upheavals. Aroden was dead, and no one on Golarion knows what was his fate. Thus this age became known as the Age of Lost Omens, as no prophecy seems to hold meaning any longer.  
The history of Golarion is vast and ancient, but this is only the beginning for this fantastical world. Many more stories are left to tell in the years to come. And these stories begin with a group of young adventurers, trying to make a name for themselves in the land of Varisia…
