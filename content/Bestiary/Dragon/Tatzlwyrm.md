A **tatzlwyrm** is a small, wingless cousin of true dragons. The slither through forest foliage and rivers, attempting to wrap themselves around their prey.

### Description
Tatzlwyrms are serpentine creatures with green or brownish scales, about 4 meters long from nose to tail, and weigh 200 kilograms. They have a row of long spines running along their back from head to tail, and sharp teeth.  

Tatzlwyrms have two small arms with claws at the end of each, allowing them to easily climb trees. They have a long, powerful and flexible tail, and most species can puff a poisonous breath.

### Ecology
A tatzlwyrm is a ferocious but cunning carnivore, lying in wait for anything from deer to men and catching them by surprise. They hunt among forests, caves, and even rivers, being able to hold their breath for hours for an ambush.  

Unlike their dragon cousins, tatzlwyrms do not keep a hoard of treasure, by they are still attracted to shiny objects, and are prone to swallow coins, river gems, and metallic objects carried by their victims. Thus, many who hunt tatzlwyrms like to cut open their bellies after the kill, in hope of finding some minor treasure the creature ate.
