Made from bones held together by foul necromancy, **skeletons** are among the most common types of undead, found haunting old dungeons and forgotten cemeteries.  

### Description
Skeletons are the husks and bones of the dead, re-animated by necrotic energies. They sometimes rise spontaneously from animal or humanoid graves, given an un-life where deathly energy coalesces. Such mindless skeletons roam the areas of their graves, compelled by their nature to kill all who wander too close. Other times, a necromancer might raise an army of shambling bones for their own nefarious reasons.

### Ecology
Skeletons retain the form of whatever creature they were animated from, and the necrotic energies that keep their bodies together allow them to move with ease despite their lack of muscles, as if keeping memories from the previous life. Humanoid skeletons might use their wrists to pick up old weapons and shields, serving as animated guards; Skeletal horses serve as intimidating mounts for other undead, while reanimated giant skeletons are much more powerful, carrying and throwing boulders.

### Society
Though mostly mindless, on rare occasions a skeleton - whether through unusual circumstances or sheer willpower - might keep aspects of their memories, personalities and free will after being reanimated. Such skeletons are not guided by the will of a master or necrotic energies, and are free to roam the world - though they might find many parts of it afraid or hateful towards them. With no place to call home, some of these skeleton might become adventurers, finding their callings on the road.
