Forged in the fires of violence and conflict, **Orcs** are a people made of strength and war above all else. Orc culture teaches that they are shaped by the challenges they survive, and the most worthy survive the most hardships. Orcs who attain both a long life and great triumphs command immense respect.  

### Description
Orcs are large humanoids, standing at an average of more than 2 meters tall, and most have broad physics with thick skeletal frame and strong musculature. Their skin tones vary by environment, from brown through various shades of grey and, commonly, different shades of green. They have strong teeth, with boar-like tusks, and large eyes which are well-adapted for seeing in the dark. They reach maturity about the same time as [[Human|humans]], but usually have lifespans of only 60 years, with many dying earlier due to their extreme environs and warring culture.   

Their rough skin scars easily, and even the youngest orc warriors bear an array of scars they wear with pride. "You are the scars that shape you" is a common aphorism among orcs. For the same reason, many orcs like to tattoo their bodies with war paints and tribal effigies.  

### Society
Most orc communities define themselves through two things: pain and glory. Each earns respect in near equal measure, so long as the pain is borne with stoicism. An orc with many scars who walks uncomplaining with a broken leg draws as much admiration as one who wins a great victory on the battlefield.   

Additionally, power defines the dynamics among families and tribes. Weaker orcs work at the behest of the strong, with power constantly shifting between orcs that prove their might. Orcs tend to share in familial duties, raising children as a community and sharing responsibilities among the entire tribe.
