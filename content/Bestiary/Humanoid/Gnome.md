Curious and eccentric, the **Gnomes** are a small folk with a distant relation to the Feyrealm. Always hungry for new experiences, gnomes view life with a child-like wonder - to them, every new day is a new adventure.  

### Description
Gnomes are small, averaging at less than a meter and weighing little more than a [[Human]] child. They have large, childlike eyes and sharp ears. The colors of their skins, eyes and hair can greatly vary from gnome to gnome, a clear indication of their Fey lineage, and all manners of colors appear in them, from the tones of the earth and the flowers to bright and flashy pinks and blues.  

Gnomes reach adulthood at the same time as humans do, but can live much longer - it is unknown exactly what is the maximum lifespan of a gnome, as most tend to perish on the road, disappear, or simply die of boredom as they reach an old enough age.  

### Society
Gnomes constantly wander, both mentally and physically. They have an innate carving for new experiences which keeps them on the move. This natural tick manifests in bizarre reasoning, eccentricity, obsessive tendencies, and what some see as naivete. Some Gnomes might bury themselves in academic research, obsessed with unlocking the secrets of magic and the universe; Others might find a deep fascination with nature, wishing to explore mother earth's beauty, and many choose to tinker with technology, building mechanical toys and knick-knacks for no other reason but to keep their hands and minds busy.  

While most gnomes adopt some of the cultural practices of the region in which they live, they tend to pick and choose, adjusting their communities to fit their own Fey logic. This often leads to majority gnome communities eventually consisting almost entirely of gnomes, as other people, bewildered by gnomish political decisions, choose to move elsewhere. Gnomes have little culture that they would consider entirely their own. Exceptionally few gnome kingdoms or nations exist on the surface of [[The World of Golarion|Golarion]], and most gnomes wouldn't know what to do with such a state if they had one.  

### Ecology
Many of Golarion's gnomes suffer from an ailment known as **The Bleaching**, an ancient Fey affliction which strikes gnomes who fail to dream, innovate, and take in new experiences. The bleaching slowly drains the color - literally - from Gnomes, and it plunges those affected into states of deep depression that eventually claim their lives. Very few gnomes survive this scourge, becoming deeply morose and wise survivors known as **bleachlings**.
