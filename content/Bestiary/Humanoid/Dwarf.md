A race of warriors and craftspeople, the stout **dwarves** are a stubborn, fierce, and devoted people. They live among mountain keeps and citadels, in harmony with rock and stone, crafting masterpieces from iron and gems.  

### Description
Dwarves are short and stocky, standing about a torso shorter than most humans. They have wide, compact bodies and burly frames. Dwarves of all genders pride themselves on the length of their hair and beards, which they often braid into intricate patterns, some of which represent specific clans. A long beard is a sign of maturity and honor among many dwarf clans.  

Dwarves typically reach physical adulthood around the age of 25, and can live to an average of 350 years old.

### Society
Though dwarven culture has greatly waned in the [[The World of Golarion#The Inner Sea|Inner Sea]], their once great empires reduced to a few small mountain kingdoms, dwarves remain stubborn to their traditions. They find passion and honor in stonework and metalwork, and the artifacts of their master smiths are adored by many. They also have old practices of war and of religion, and many dwarves devote their life to these traditions.  

Dwarves usually live in clans containing multiple families. For most dwarves, the communal connection to their clan is sacred, and to be outcast from one's clan is the greatest shame a dwarf may experience. For outsiders who would harm a clan, they may find dwarves are as stubborn as the rock and have memories unaffected by the turn of time. They can hold grudges for ages, not content until the enemy repays for their ancient mistakes.
