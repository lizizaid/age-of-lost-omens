Countless legends tell of mortal dealings with beings of the greater [[Cosmology|cosmos]], and of the children born from those encounters. These children, known as **Nephilim** or **Planar Scions**, are mortals who inherit the supernatural [[Cosmology#Essences|Essence]] of another plane, which manifests through their distinctive physical features and otherworldly powers. 

### Description
The cosmic touch may appear in any mortal race. While their base appearance is similar to that of their birth ancestry, Each Nephilim is unique, with traits received from their planar origin. For example, Nephilim with a touch of the [[Cosmology#The Elemental Planes|Elemental Planes]] might have a rocky texture for their skin or a hair made of pure flame. Those who are touched by the [[Cosmology#Upper Planes|Upper Planes]] might have glowing metallic eyes or small halos over their heads, while those who are touched by the [[Cosmology#Lower Planes|Lower Planes]] might have bestial hooves, demonic horns and long fangs.  

### Ecology
A faithful priest of [[Erastil]] is visited by an angel in his youth, and many years later he fathers a child with the horns of an elk and a birthmark shaped like a bow and arrow. A powerful sorceress prevents an incursion from the Abyss, but a sliver of demonic power infuses the child she carries, who is born with fangs and a pair of tiny horns. A planar caravan travels the cosmos until it's ranks are filled with children who possess chaotic magics and skin tones of all the colors of the rainbow. Each of these examples produced a Nephilim, on one hand possessing the ambition and capacity for growth and change inherent in their mortal forebears, on the other possessing a portion of incredible cosmic power and entities who infused their bloodline.  
### Society
As Nephilim could be born into any ancestry and land, the way they make their way through society might change drastically. For example, in a holy kingdom with pious citizens, people who are born as [[Nephilim, Aasimar|Aasimars]] might be revered and worshiped as children of the Celestials, encouraged by society to embrace the goodness flowing in their veins and become heroes and guardians, while [[Nephilim, Tiefling|Tieflings]] might be looked at in shame and disgust as the spawn of Fiends, pushed to the fringes of society and made one with their base evil as criminals and outcasts. However, Nephilim are mortal beings, and as any mortals are not defined by their bloodlines, but by how society and their actions shape them. An Aasimar in that holy kingdom might crumble and break under society's expectations, becoming tempted in vice and sin as a way to escape, while a Tiefling might find resolve in their burden, working hard to prove the people they too possess good in their heart.  

### Types of Nephilim
The many Planes of the Multiverse and their inhabitants produce many types of Scions, including:
- [[Nephilim, Aasimar|Aasimars]], scions of Celestials of the [[Cosmology#Upper Planes|Upper Planes]]
- [[Nephilim, Tiefling|Tieflings]], scions of Fiends of the [[Cosmology#Lower Planes|Lower Planes]]
