## Abadar
*Master of The First Vault*  
![250](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/pathfinderkingmaker/images/3/34/Abadar.jpg)

**Domains** cities, law, merchants, wealth  
**Edicts** bring civilization to the frontiers, earn wealth through hard work and trade, follow the rule of law  
**Anathemas** engage in banditry or piracy, steal, undermine a law-abiding court  
**Holy Symbol** a golden key engraved with the image of the first vault

**Abadar** (pronounced AH-bah-dar), the god of cities, law, merchants, and wealth, is known to be a patient deity. Maintaining a strong neutral stance in his actions, he sets forth to expand civilization and order among the peoples of Golarion.

### Dogma
Abadar’s worship is commonplace anywhere civilization holds sway. His cathedral banks are found in every major city on Golarion, while smaller temples pop up in frontier outposts and trading hubs. He’s as popular with community-minded common folk, labor organizers, and craftspeople as he is with wealthy aristocrats, legal professionals, city guards, and merchants. City-bound Abadaran clerics are often judges, lawyers, and clerks, while their more adventurous counterparts often act as roving merchants, ensuring the flow of essential commerce from one region to another, or as traveling magistrates, acting as judges in the name of order.  

Abadar and his followers wish to bring the light of civilization to the wilderness, to help educate all in the benefits of law and properly regulated commerce. He expects his followers to obey all meaningful laws, but not those which are ridiculous, unenforceable, or self-contradictory. He is also a great proponent of peace, as war inevitably leads to the degradation of trade and the stifling of prosperity for the general public. He advocates cautious, careful consideration in all matters, and frowns on impulsiveness, believing that it leads to the encouragement of primitive needs.  

Abadar discourages dependence on government or any religious institution, believing that wealth and happiness should be achievable by anyone with keen judgment, discipline, and a healthy respect for all sensible, just laws.  


### Mythology
Abadar is an old deity, worshiped since humans have first built cities and walls. The stories claim he was one of the original gods who battled the Rough Beast. After [[Gorum]] and [[Torag]] forged the shell of his prison, the Dead Vault, Abadar provided the perfect key and lock for it. 
##### Relationships
Abadar strives for a balance in all things, including his divine relationships. He finds the chaos of unpredictable bedfellows odious and often downright dangerous. He courts the favor of righteous deities such as [[Iomedae]] as much as he does the basest of fiends like [[Asmodeus]]. [[Torag]], Shelyn, Brigh, Shizuru, and [[Erastil]] all enjoy alliances with the Judge of the Gods. Deities who embody or pursue agendas of order and perfection find common ground with Abadar as they work together to foster growth and expansion. Cultural exchange is one of the cornerstones of stable civilizations, so Abadar encourages his worshipers to study comparative religion in allied temples.  

The combined works of Abadaran priests and those of other gods are legends made manifest. Collaborations with priests of Brigh have produced some of the most opulent and ornate clockwork wonders the world has seen. The gothic spires of the Abadaran cathedral bank loom confidently side by side with Asmodean temples in the tyrannical capital of infernal Cheliax. The stained glass gifted by Abadaran priests to Iomedae’s followers upon her ascension is said to be among the most beautiful in the world, with some observers claiming it to be the closest thing they’ll come to directly seeing the face of the divine. Worshippers of [[Torag]] often collaborate with Abadaran merchants to sell their fine steel, fetching the best price for both parties.  

Although he does not actively oppose wicked gods, and has been known to forge alliances with the more order-minded among them, Abadar reserves a special ire for those that would tear down civilization, disrupt commerce, and destroy great works. Abadar would have it told that it was he that brought gods of maleficence and charity together to battle Rovagug (his only true enemy), though that fact is up for some deb ate among those present at the Age of Creation. He loathes Lamashtu, considering her an evil just under Rovagug—less of a threat but a more consistent and active thorn in his side. Norgorber is generally more of an annoyance to Abadar than anything, but he knows that the secrets Norgorber holds could end an empire faster than a giant’s club or dragon’s fire. Gozreh often opposes Abadar’s more remote forays in civil works but, much to Gozreh’s consternation, Abadar hardly acknowledges their presence and generally continues his work unabated.
##### Realm
Abadar's divine realm is Aktun, a massive city at the heart of Axis. The city is an architectural marvel, built of many circled rings containing inter-dimensional markets, banks and cathedrals. At the center of the city lies the First Vault, Abadar's personal palace and repository said to contain a perfect version of every thing ever created or seen by civilized people, such as the perfect sword, the perfect song or the prefect human. 
##### Servitors
Abadar's divine servants consist of many outsiders who have a commitment to stability and prosperity, mainly consisting of archons but also including aeons, agathions, angels and even a few stray devils.

As exhaustively detailed as the edicts are within Abadar's holy book, *The Order of Numbers*, they can be boiled down into a rather simple philosophy: perfection is for the gods alone, but its pursuit is the true path to the divine. Pursue perfection by fostering growth and community through hard work and commerce, by fostering order through adherence to the law, and by fostering justice through working tirelessly to improve it where it falls short.  

Abadar is primarily worshiped by judges, merchants, lawyers, and aristocrats, though many others turn to the worship of the Master of the First Vault in the hope of attaining wealth and happiness. He is also an object of veneration by the poor, or those who have suffered at the hands of others. These latter groups pray that their suffering is alleviated and that justice is restored to them.  

Abadar's faithful believe structures represent the protection provided by society and always hold formal ceremonies indoors. Regular services are generally held in the morning and often involve songs which require the congregation to harmonize, which may symbolize the community working together. Lay worshipers will maintain the appearance of prosperity in manner and dress and, even in periods of hardship, a tidy appearance.  
##### Adventurers
Adventuring priests of Abadar seek to bring order an defend civilization wherever it is found. They are lawmen, pioneers, and bulwarks against the forces of darkness and disorder. Quests they might embark on include defending a frontiers settlement from monsters, reprisal of artifacts from long-lost civilizations, or recording a dying language before it is lost to time.  


### Church
The Abadaran church is highly regimented and hierarchical. Whether it’s the destitute commoner who diligently tithes, the judge dedicated to meting out justice, or the merchant prince who moves goods through the process of supply and demand, every member understands their role in the church, their duties, and their obligations. While all manner of divine worship is common throughout Golarion, Abadar’s permeation throughout the political and economic landscape of nearly every city makes his presence hard to ignore. From passing and enforcing laws to collecting taxes to sponsoring community growth, public opinion of Abadaran priests varies wildly throughout the year.  

His fortified temples often act as banks or trading hubs, depending on the size of the city. Despite the brutalist structures, his temples often bear beautiful stained-glass windows and are constructed of white marble, if available, and gilded with gold. These temples stand as monuments to wealth and prosperity wherever they are.  

##### Religious Texts
With the clergy of Abadar often serving their communities as clerks, a given temple is likely to have extensive records within its vaults. Among these many documents, the following texts have primary importance among the god of cities' faithful:
- **The Order of Numbers**: Usually bearing elaborate decoration, writing, and binding, this book serves as the core text of the Church of Abadar.
- **The Manual of City-Building**: Often stored prominently in a place of honor, this text contains advice on the topic of founding and running a community.

##### Holidays
- **Taxfest** (15th of Gozran): While paying taxes is an activity enjoyed by approximately no one, Abadaran priests consider taxation an essential keystone in the grand arch of civilization. Abadaran priests know how odious this day can be for the common folk and endeavor to make it as lively, vibrant, and distracting as possible.
- **Market's Door**: This day, which varies from year to year, marks the arrival of the first shipment of goods from the fall harvest.

##### Aphorisms
- **By Abadar's gaze, the deal is sealed**: When a deal is struck and finalized, the initiating party often utters this phrase to indicate negotiations have concluded. Often shorted to “By Abadar’s gaze,” or “The deal is sealed,” attempts to alter the deal after the phrase is uttered are universally frowned upon.
- **So it is judged**: A standard conclusion to official legal verdicts. Some followers use this phrase when random events seemingly work out in favor of what they believe to be justice.
- **They have Abadar's eyes for...**: A compliment, describing a person with a keen sense of perfection in their chosen field.
