## Torag
*Father of Creation*  
![150](https://pathfinderwiki.com/w/images/a/a7/Torag_symbol.png)

**Domains** forge, dwarves, protection, strategy  
**Edicts** be honorable and forthright, keep your word, respect the forge, serve your people  
**Anathemas** tell lies or cheat someone, intentionally create inferior works, show mercy to the enemies of your people   
**Holy Symbol** a metal hammer

"Subtle as a hammer" is a phrase commonly uttered to describe not only the worshipers of **Torag** (pronounced TORR-awg), but also the god himself. A stoic and serious deity, Torag is the father and patron of the [[Dwarf|dwarven]] people of the [[The World of Golarion#The Inner Sea|Inner Sea]] region, and head of the dwarven pantheon. He teaches his followers, whether dwarf or not, the virtues of patience and steadfastness, but also commands them to protect their community and show no mercy to those they hold grudge against.  

### Dogma
Torag counts craftspeople among his most ardent followers, be they workers of metal, stone, or cloth. He guides them in their craft, and encourages them to create masterpieces. For Torag, honest and effective work is a sacred art - each hit of the hammer on the anvil a divine hymn, each stone statue carved a religious artifact. Torag's followers take this idea to heart, finding joy and inspiration in the craft. Many of his simple believers take up a craft as a hobby or profession, but the creations of his greatest priests are magnificent artifacts sought after by collectors from all over the world.  

Not to be outshone, soldiers and mercenaries often pray to Torag in his aspect as protector and strategist, praying to harm will come to them or their companions as they take the fight to those who wish to hurt the people they will defend. While not a merciful god, Torag the warrior teaches his battle wisdom only for those who would use it to protect, never for glory or gain.  

Community is foremost among Torag's dogmas, and he believes true strength comes from a bedrock of support - the clan or the family - at the base of the worshiper. Toragian community traditions are rooted deep in dwarven culture, and give many followers a sense of identity - while for some, these rigid rules and traditionalist values may be too suffocating, most find comfort and warmth in their communities, a place of their own in an often cruel and unfriendly world.  


### Mythology
The dwarves believe that Torag created the world at his great forge, striking it again and again with his hammer to get the shape desired. As rocks tumbles and sparks flew, the dwarves were born, bodies made of stone with bellies filled with fire.  

Torag was among the original gods who battled the Rough Beast. He and [[Gorum]] forged the shell of the Dead Vault, which would serve as Rovagug's prison after his defeat.  

##### Relationships
As a deity of both creation and protection, Torag opposes destruction simply for its own sake, as well as aggression with no purpose. He stands in direct opposition to Rovagug, and his followers will travel far and wide to battle the cultists of that god as well as the supremely destructive Spawn. Interestingly, followers of Torag tend to find followers of Sarenrae off-putting. Dwarves view a reliance on the sun as strange, and the Sarenites’ quickness to forgive is considered a weakness. [[Erastil]] shares many aspects in common with Torag, as both value community and family as important pillars of the faith. Torag respects [[Abadar]] for his emphasis on order, as well as the edicts involving hard work and trade, both of which aid in creation. While not sharing many, if any, aspects, Torag is friendly with [[Iomedae]], as well as [[Cayden Cailean]], though the former’s edicts of glory and fame run counter to Torag’s core beliefs.  

Torag resides in Forgeheart with the other members of the dwarven pantheon, who are his close-knit family. While a person can worship a given member of the pantheon exclusively, it’s not uncommon for followers to give prayers to Torag and other members of his family at once. Torag understands that although he’s the head of his family, there might be things that his followers can’t receive from him and doesn’t bear ill will to worshippers who also turn to other members of his family. So long as these followers respect Torag and hold him with the appropriate amount of regard, he is content to share divine influence with his family.
##### Realm
Torag and his family reside in Forgeheart, a colossal fortress in Heaven. Specifically, Forgeheart is carved into the heart of the mountain of Heaven. Countless tunnels and caverns make up the majority of Forgeheart, with each cave said to be the center of a given craft or trade, such as a cavern dedicated entirely to the production of perfect leather straps for shields. The forges glow day and night without fail, crafting the weapons and armor needed to outfit the armies of Heaven as well as acting as a bulwark against any and all incursions.  
##### Servitors
Torag and his faithful hold creatures of the earth as mighty and holy, especially burrowing animals like badgers. His armies of protectors are made up of archons and angels, as well as good creatures of elemental earth.  

### Followers

Followers of Torag follow the god’s tenets with fanatical devotion. Those who pay more than lip service to the god will strive to create, working with their hands as an artisan of some craft or another. Smiths are the most numerous, but gem cutting, smelting, woodwork, tanning, or leatherwork are also considered acceptable crafts, taking both years to master and considered useful or something of beauty. The forge, or the hearth, can represent the solid core of the community in which the follower lives.  

Proper care for the forge means ensuring that the local community flourishes, is protected from those who would do it harm, and can grow and expand. To this end, Torag worshipers will find themselves involved in all aspects of the local economy, from smithing to trade to local defense. Battle strategists who pay homage pray for wisdom in battle, the placing of defensive structures, and the foresight to anticipate the enemy plans of battle to defeat them.  

As devotees to a god so involved in acts of creation, the clergy wear simple utilitarian garb: a well-crafted apron, with slots for the tools of their chosen craft, are all a priests needs. Rings, badges, or other ornaments that signify important life achievements such as births, marriages, deaths, or the first forging of a tool are often their only decoration. Lay people among Torag’s worshipers are just as often craftspeople as well. They respect the desire for mastering a difficult craft and honing their skills over long years. Knights in the service of Torag value action, and the personal growth that comes from perfecting the art of combat against ones enemies and in defense of one’s home.  
##### Adventurers
Adventurers who worship Torag set out not for glory or treasure, but usually for more practical reasons. Some of these worshippers set out to find rare materials for their community, to seek tutelage form master crafters, or some other task on behalf of their community or temple. As tradition is important among Torag’s followers, some adventurers set out to reclaim lost relics or misplaced knowledge important to a community. Some of these adventurers also travel to places to serve as reinforcements for places under siege or at war. Within an adventuring group, many followers of Torag take on leadership role, or at least offer counsel to a group’s leader in the form of strategy.  


### Church
Churches of Torag are often the beating heart of any settlement. A large central forge surrounded in concentric circles of satellite anvils allows the masters to work on the most important or most delicate creations, and allows the apprentices to both work on their own projects while watching the masters and learning from their combined experiences. Creation and commerce are the building blocks that allow any grouping of individuals to survive. Prayers to Torag are sent up with every blow of the hammer on the anvil, every blow of the axe on the tree, and every bite of the pick to the earth.  

Anywhere there is craft can be a church to the Forgefather, although traditionally it will be a metalworking forge. While most Torag holy sites are at forges within settlements, anywhere that his presence can be felt will become the site of pilgrimages. These sites then benefit from a constant influx of traveling craftspeople, for whom quality goods or skills are a religious necessity. Oftentimes creation, crafting, or otherwise working material is an integral part of the services. In some of the larger settlements, the very pillars upon which the building rests will be carved with images of hammer, forge, or a stern dwarven visage. In smaller communities, a simple carved bowl or small statuette may have to suffice.  

Each community will have their own special methods or daily ablutions, but those are followed to the letter. Tradition and stability are the watchwords of the church, as it celebrates those who have spent long decades mastering their chosen craft and encouraging those less experienced to do the same. The Master Smith of the community serves as the local high priest, overseeing life events of the faithful including births, marriages, and deaths.  

Churches to Torag often serve as fallback positions, or sturdy strongholds for those unable to defend themselves. In outdoor settlements, it’s not uncommon to see the forge temples built into the very defensive walls that surround the region. This has the benefit of keeping the constant clanging noises away from the other residents and an easy path for the Torag clergy to monitor the surrounding lands for threats, as well as the integrity of the outer walls, as allowing defensive structures to fall into disrepair is anathema to Torag’s beliefs.  

##### Religious Texts
Torag's texts tend to be sturdily bound tomes, able to survive whatever hardships its owner may face.
- **Hammer and Thongs**: The principle religious text of Torag, this tome contains the creation myths of the dwarves and the destinies they have forged. In addition to prayers and rituals, it also includes instructions on how to shape stone, build walls, smelt base metals, and forge iron and steel. It is also common for a copy to include basic information about various predatory monsters and how to defeat them, especially for creatures in the local region.

##### Holidays
Different for each community or member of the faith, most favored holidays of Torag involve remembrance of great battles of importance, where it is felt that their memory must be preserved. Oaths to never forget, to protect the existing or future communities, and to honor fallen family are commonly sworn on such days.  

##### Aphorisms
- **By Kaglemros!**: Torag’s holy hammer is invoked when the follower of Torag sees a creation of great beauty, something to be admired. Alternatively, it can be used as a ward against the perceived bad luck of seeing something of inferior make, or the consequences of broken or misused equipment.
- **Useless as a lock in Forgeheart**: Forgeheart, the citadel in Heaven from which Torag tends to his forge and supplies armaments to the ranks of Heavens army, is a monstrous behemoth of defensive magnificence, and only a truly foolhardy individual seeking a swift death would attempt to sneak into such a place. It’s a common belief that no door, chest, or strongbox contains a lock on it, as the only individuals who reside there have Torag’s utmost trust.
