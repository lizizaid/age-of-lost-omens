## Droksar
*The Dark Smith*  
![250](https://i.postimg.cc/yxZ064Tf/droskars-holy-symbol.png)

**Domains** toil, cheating, exploitation, forges    
**Edicts** achieve goals at any cost, continually improve your abilities, establish dominance, work ceaselessly  
**Anathemas** fail to work toward goals or grow in skill, relax excessively or give in to sloth  
**Holy Symbol** fire under a stone arch

**Droskar** (pronounced DROH-skar), the god of endless toil, is the ancient enemy of the dwarven pantheon and patron of the hryngars, the dark dwarves. His followers believe that constant, unrewarding work is the only way to prove their worth in their dark master's eyes, and they do not shy from cheating and enslaving to further their goals.

### Dogma
Droskar is a hard and unforgiving god, his relatively simple tenets enforced with brutal efficiency. He teaches that success and safety come only through unceasing toil, and that work purifies the soul—lessons that prepare the spirits of his followers to aid him in the Ashen Forge for eternity. For the Dark Smith, the ends always justify the means, and if you fail at something, it means you weren’t ruthless or diligent enough.  

On the other hand, Droskar also acknowledges that you can achieve much by swindling and treachery. He supports slavery and the exploitation of others, as forcing others to perform drudgery allows you to achieve more than you could alone, while also freeing you up to work on the more pleasant or creative aspects of your pursuits. He disdains sloth, however, and rulers who grow soft through the efforts of their thralls deserve to be overthrown by those beneath them who’ve been hardened in the crucible of labor. His priests venerate hard work and the skills that come through repetition, teaching that greatness is never inborn, but rather achieved through pushing yourself (and your underlings) to the absolute limit. Success is always better than failure, and thus his followers are free to do whatever is necessary to achieve their goals—including lying, cheating, theft, and murder.

### Mythology
Devout dwarves tend not to speak of the Dark Smith, who acts as a sort of boogeyman in dwarven religion. When stories about him are told, dwarf mythology claims that Droskar was once a member of the dwarven pantheon - a master blacksmith, and [[Torag|Torag's]] best and brightest student. Torag loved his apprentice as a son, until he discovered a terrible truth - Droskar has faked his brilliance, instead enslaving another deity and forcing them to create masterpieces, which he took credit for. Ashamed, Torag banished Droskar from the dwarven halls, cursing him to never again be able to create an original work.  

The hryngars build on this mythos, telling that in his exile, Droskar has found their lost souls screaming for salvation. Abandoned by Torag, just like he was, Droskar have adopted the dark dwarves as his children, demanding of them absolute obedience and eternal servitude in exchange for his fiery warmth.    
##### Relationships
Droskar’s relationship with the rest of the dwarven pantheon is strained, to say the least, and the same goes for their respective churches. Most dwarves abhor Droskar and go out of their way to ostracize or attack his faithful, and the Droskari eagerly enslave them in return.  
##### Realm
Droskar's realm in Gehenna is known as the Ashen Forge, an endless tangle of mine shafts and forges that worm through the plane's stone, constantly collapsing and being rebuilt by his minions in an endless cycle of misery. Enslaves artisan souls toil without end at the forges, while warriors stand their ground at subterranean fortresses, tirelessly fighting invading fiends and celestials alike. The Dark Smith himself lounges in the cavern's center upon the Forge Throne, a seat formed from the the shrieking souls of those who've disobeyed him.   
##### Servitors
Droskar's servitors include legions of fiendish dwarves, dark constructs, and broken undead spirits bound to his service.

Followers of Droskar believe that work is sacred, and those who do not work are blasphemers. They toil endlessly, creating tools for practicality only, and never for artistic value. For the hryngars, Droskar's church is a state religion, and any member who is caught worshiping other deities is immediately persecuted and put to work in the slave force, or worse.  

Priests of Droskar act as stern parents to parishioners, extolling the value of hard work and the necessity of toil to secure a more comfortable role in Droskar’s afterlife. They preach hierarchy but lead through example, driving themselves as hard as those beneath them. While a priest of Droskar may have other vocations, their primary job is always to spread the Dark Smith’s influence.


### Church
From simple smithies to massive cathedral-factories, Droskar’s temples are places of labor and crafting. In societies where his faithful must worship in secret, a shrine might be nothing more than his holy symbol etched into the bottom of a farrier’s anvil, so that every strike is secretly a prayer to the Dark Smith. Among hryngar and other openly devout congregations, however, his churches incorporate huge foundries, mills, quarries, or other such places, often forming the industrial heart of a given settlement.

##### Religious Texts
- **Book of Chains**: Given to a hryngar prophet in a vision of flame, Droskar's tenets are compiled in this short text, often inscribed on metal slabs. Its most famous verse says: "There is freedom in slavery. Work strengthens. Repetition teaches. By denying us the option of weakness, a master perfects us. In the fires of our labor, we are reforged."  

##### Holidays
- **Tithe**: The only major Droskarian holiday, the week of Tithe resolves around work and worship. Masters gather to showcase and trade their great works, claim new apprentices, and participate in public crafting contests. All proceeds, of course, go to the church's treasury.  

##### Aphorisms
- **Fold the steel**: A common command and comfort, this phrase refers to the process of forging steel, in which repeated folding evens out impurities and weak points to create a stronger blade. As a command, it urges the faithful to continue toiling, as each repetition of a task increases their skill and resilience. Yet it can also be a comfort to the suffering, comparing each loss they suffer to a hammer blow from the Dark Smith, strengthening the steel of their souls.
- **Any road to glory**: This admonishment is a reminder that Droskar doesn’t care how you succeed, only that you do so. While used as a justification by thieves and cheats, it also guides rulers to break treaties, sacrifice troops, or otherwise focus on utilitarian rather than moral concerns in the name of victory and advancement.
