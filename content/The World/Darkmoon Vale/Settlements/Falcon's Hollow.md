### Info
Remote lumber town, ruled by a self-interested consortium.  
- **Size** small town
- **Government** council (Lumber Consortium)
- **Population** 2,400 (90% human, %5 dwarf, %3 halfling, %2 other)
- **Languages** Common, Dwarven
- **Religions** Lip service to various faiths
- **Trade Goods** darkwood, furniture, hunted game, furs
- **Traits** 
	- Lumber town - Those who come to Falcon’s Hollow can always find work in the cutyards or lumber mills. A character can Earn Income in Falcon’s Hollow by attempting Athletics or Labor Lore, and if they do, they can attempt up to a 6th-level task.

### Description
Falcon's Hollow is one in a line of lumber towns constructed in [[Darkmoon Vale]] by the greedy Lumber Consortium, It's sole purpose to plunder the woodland's resources. The town's citizens are mostly lumbers and hunters, struggling daily with the burdens of hard physical work, constant attacks by forest monsters, and the oppressive auspices of the Consortium. Nevertheless, the resilient folk of Falcon's Hollow find grim pride in their work. They're a rough and honest (for the most part) people who generally don't turn to heroes for saving - their problems are their own.

### History
Falcon's Hollow, along with its sister colonies, were built by human settlers from neighboring kingdoms about two hundred years ago. The noble families leading the settlers soon banded together to become the Lumber Consortium, with the brutal Kreed family ruling Falcon's Hollow throughout the years.  
The hard people of the Hollow endured many devastating monster attacks throughout the centuries, including the Harpy Massacre of 4603 AR, the Undead Misery of 4631 AR and the Hobgoblin Strife of 4679 AR. Most recently, rumors of kobolds attacking and kidnapping citizens have started to bubble.

### Geography
Falcon's Hollow is located just at the edge of the Darkmoon Wood, a fact that is easy to see in the town's architecture - most buildings are made of dark logs from the forest's tress.  The volcano [[Droskar's Crag]] dominates the skyline to the northwest. The [[River Foam]] acts as the southern boundary of Falcon's Hollow, and ferries often dock here, loading chopped wood to take downstream. 

### Government
Life in Falcon's Hollow revolves around the harvesting of darkwood, and as such the Lumber Consortium holds sway over the community. The Consortium's primary representative in the area, Thuldrin Kreed, maintains control of the town by denying goods and services through means other than those he allows. Firmly believing his hold on the town to be complete, Kreed has no qualms against exploiting his power and position for personal gain, often at the expense of the citizenry.

### Economy
An old and established logging community, most of Falcon's Hollow economy is based on the export of wood via the Lumber Consortium to the neighboring human and dwarven kingdoms. A minority of it's citizens include hunters and trappers, as well as artisans, especially those who build wooden furniture.  
Additionally, the town attracts many fresh adventurers, who set out in order to find riches in the region's many dwarven ruins.

### Inhabitants
Most of Falcon's Hollow inhabitants are humans, with some dwarf and halfling families living among them as well. Citizens of the town include:
- Thuldrin Kreed
- Sherrif Deldrin Baleson
- [[Lady Cirthana Gensar]]

### Locations
1. **Temple of Iomedae**
		A small darkwood chapel, the temple of [[Iomedae]] is the town's only place of worship. Though not many of the town's residents come to pray, and despite the Kreed family tackling her any attempts to reform them, the temple's keeper, [[Lady Cirthana Gensar]], keeps doing her best to bring her goddesses justice and light to this gloomy settlment.
2. **Jak A' Napes**
		A ramshackle inn, offering food and lodging to travelers passing through Falcon's Hollow. The ruddy inkeeper, Jak Crimmy, always has a warm welcome, a solid meal, and a tale or two for weary visitors. The inn is most known for it's cinnamon pastries, especially Jak's legendary cinnamon pancakes she makes once per week, which the line to taste a sugared forkful runs all the way around the building and down the street.
3. **Low Market**
		Open to all, the Low Market sells all sorts of leftovers, including produce no longer fresh enough for High Market's favored costumers, heavily salted meats and fish, and other questionable foodstuff. Weapons, armor and other gear is available, but never of a good quality. Hordes of poor woodcutters and struggling hunters frequent the Low Market. A few ramshackle stalls serve ale and blindness-inducing moonshine to carousing loggers, fueling their revels and making picking pockets an easy prospect.
4. **High Market**
		Located on a small hill, literally above most of the town, High Market's greatest draw is the rot-free meats and vegetables for sale. Weekly caravans run to and from the larger town of Olfden, stocking the market with silk clothing, shoes, spices, and jewelry. Local artists also sell their wares here, and it is one of the few reliable places in Falcon's Hollow where weapons, armor and more can be purchased.  However, the market's wares aren't available to all. Consortium thugs restrict access, admitting only those Thuldrin deems influential.
