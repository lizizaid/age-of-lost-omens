A backwater wilderness region by any other measure, **Varisia's** only claim for fame is it's rich history. Being the site for the ancient mageocracy of [[Thassilon]], Varisia is full of arcane ruins, dangerous monsters, and powerful artifacts, thus making this realm a hotbed for adventurers and heroes alike.


# Geography
Varisia is a vast and sparsely populated region in the southwest of the [[The World of Golarion#Saga Lands|Saga Lands]]. It is bordered by the Steaming Sea in the west and the Mindspin Mountains to the east. The northern coastline, where some of Varisia's larger ports can be found, is also known as [[The Lost Coast]], so named for it's fog-laden vastness and isolation.  

Geographically, Varisia is split into two regions: the south and west are generally lowlands with a few forested mountains along with rich grasslands, forests, and swamps; The northeastern section is dominated by harsh and dry badlands and rocky terrain.


# History
### Thassilonian Empire
Over 10,000 years ago, much of modern day Varisia was ruled by one the greatest civilizations in the history of the [[The World of Golarion#The Inner Sea|Inner Sea]] - the empire of [[Thassilon]]. Formed by [[Azlant|Azlanti]] exiles, and ruled by Runelords, wizards of tremendous power who practiced sin magic, this nation built enormous magical monuments to it's own vanity using enslaved giants and dragons. Many of the lower caste people of this kingdom lived in fear and misery, subject to the endless wars the Runelords wrought upon each other.  

Already weakened by corruption and warfare, Thassilon finally succumbed to destruction during the events of [[Earthfall]]. The runelords, having foreseen this apocalypse, hid in numerous protected sanctuaries to escape death, entering a deep sleep in which they lay for millennia.

### Age of Darkness
With the Runelords powerless and slumbering, the surviving people of Varisia were left to create their own destiny. During the decades, the surviving human population of Thassilon has splinted into two cultural groups: the larger group adopted the name **Varisians** (from which the current name of the region is adapted), and have took on a nomadic lifestyle. The smaller group became the **Shoanti**, who settled in small tribes and developed a shamanic and warrior-based culture.  

### The Great Expansion
Varisia was mostly ignored by the re-emerging powers of the Inner Sea for thousands of years, seen mainly as yet another monster-infested swamp with no real reasons to colonize the place. However, in the last two hundred years, that sentiment has slowly started to change; First came settlers and exiles from nearby kingdoms, looking for a fresh start in a new land. After them, came the adventurers and treasure hunters, looking to make a profit by raiding the ancient Thassilonian ruins and securing their artifacts. This expansion of other cultures into Varisia has not been without conflict with the local populace, but as the years went by, the settlers have managed to create some points of civilization within the wilderness. These days, most of the inhabitants of Varisia do not consider themselves aligned with one kingdom or another, and are simply Varisians - and are usually fierce defenders of their home.


# Inhabitants
### Humans
Most [[Human|humans]] in current-day Varisia are descendant of settles from the Great Expansion, with a large percent of the population having mixed heritage with the local populace.  

##### Varisians
A portion of the human population still consider themselves pure **Varisians**. Due to their tendency to travel, they can be found all across the land, from the big cities to small villages and even many nomadic caravans.  
Varisian people typically possess deep, olive-colored skin, and long hair that comes in an in a large range of colors from platinum to the darkest black. They often have large, expressive eyes and lithe bodies with long limbs. They often grab themselves in colorful and bright clothing, and are fond of scarves of all types.  
Apart from a culture of traveling the open road, the Varisians have deep traditions of performance, the mystical arts, and Harrow readings; Due to their unique customs, they are often depicted as thieves and scoundrels among other peoples. Despite this, Varisian tend to be a genuine people, loyal to family and friends.  

##### Shoanti
The **Shoanti** are tribal people living in clans, mostly in the eastern, arid areas of Varisia. Compared to other populations, they tend to be thought as a barbaric and primitive culture, and though they are not violent by nature, they do have a warrior culture that emphasizes protection of the clan above all.  
Shoanti skin tones are usually dark brown, and most have strong builds and a ruddy complexion.  
Shoanti live in animistic clans and worship totems, ancestral spirits, and traditional gods such as [[Desna]], [[Gorum]], [[Gozreh]] and [[Pharasma]]. Their barbarian warriors are known for their fierceness, traditionally wielding Earthbreaker hammers and Klar blades. They constantly get in conflict with orcs, giants, and other monsters which plague their homelands.  

### Dwarves
The [[Dwarf|Dwarven]] people have settled in the Mindspin Mountains of Varisia decades ago, slowly building the great citadel-city of [[Janderhoff]]. Today, most of their population can still be found in those mountains, though pockets of dwarven families and clans can be found all throughout the land, working as mercenaries, merchants, and hunters.  
Varisian dwarves tend to have tanned skins and hair of deep, earthen tones. They have a reputation of being fiercely loyal, and usually get along well with people of other ancestries.

### Elves
Though an entire [[Elf|Elvish]] kingdom existed in Varisia prior to to Earthfall, only a handful of Elves live here now, scattered mostly in small tribes in the various woods of the region. They live from the land as hunters and gathered, and have very little interaction with other cultures. Other elves live among humans and dwarves, mixed with their populations throughout the years.  
Varisian elves usually have light skins with a shade of purplish-pink in them, and many sport light hair colors. Many are adept at climbing and navigating the forests. They are considered haughty and mysterious by others.

### Halflings
[[Halfling|Halfling]] are an adaptable lot, and though not native to Varisia, a large population of them arrived with the human settlers of the Great Expansion. Many work as sailors, scratching their itch for exploration by travelling the seas and rivers of Varisia, while others a content with settling down in simple homes in the countryside.  

### Goblins
The [[Goblin]] population of Varisia is infamous for their violent natures and low intelligence. They live in tribes all along the Varisian countryside, and constantly get in conflict with other civilizations, raiding and stealing with glee. These traits have made goblins hated by most people of Varisia - however, they are not inherently bound to these violent traditions, and some have successfully integrated into other cultures.  
Varisian goblins have sickly green skin, round bald heads and large, beady red eyes. What small traditions these goblins keep amount to a love of singing (but a distrust of writing), an unexplained hatred of dogs and horses, worship of [[Lamashtu]], and a deep fascination with fire and burning things.

### Dragons
Although dragons of many kinds have dwelt in Varisia since time immemorial, until recently they have had few encounters with the smaller humanoid races, content to hunt the wilderness and bicker among themselves. As the humanoid population centers have grown and expanded in recent years, so have the encounters with dragons.

### Giants
In eons past, the Runelords have exploited the expertise and power of Varisia's giant population in order to build their cities and monuments. Thousands of years later, countless giants still inhabit Varisia, although most have retreated to the mountains and hills in the east. These include tribes of Hill and Stone Giants, as well as many other types, such as the unique Runelord-created Rune Giants.

### Ogres
The ogres of Varisia are some of the most notorious in the Inner Sea region. Their destructive tribes serve as a kind of buffer between the settled people of the lower plains the giants of the plateaus. As populations slowly expands in Varisia, it is only a matter of time before ogres are pushed into open warfare, but for now they are happy arguing and fighting amongst themselves.
