### Info
A beacon of civilization in a wild frontier, and [[Varisia|Varisia's]] most important trading port.
- **Size** large city
- **Government** constitutional monarchy
- **Population** 48,000 (79% [[Human]], 6% [[Dwarf]], 4% [[Elf]], 3% [[Halfling]], %3 [[Elf, Half Elf|Half Elf]], %2 [[Gnome]], %3 other)
- **Languages** Common, Varisian
- **Religions** [[Abadar]], [[Asmodeus]], [[Pharasma]], [[Sarenrae]], [[Shelyn]]
- **Resources** seafood, imported goods, magic items
- **Traits** 
	- Maritime industries - People looking for work in Korvosa usually go to the city docks and fisheries, as more helping hands in the cities shipping and seafood industries are always needed. A character can Earn Income in Korvosa by attempting Athletics or Sailing Lore, and if they do, they can attempt up to a 6th-level task. 
	- Arcane learning - The Acadamae and many smaller schools teach arcane arts in Korvosa. Many graduates remain in the city, leading to a thriving trade in arcane items and information. Arcane magical items are more common in shops, along with most components for spells and rituals.


### Description
Proudly wearing it's heritage of hard-working settlers and pioneers with imperial red flags on every building, tower, and rooftop, the city of Korvosa is a shining beacon of civilization in the wild frontiers of [[Varisia]]. The oldest and biggest human settlement in the [[The Lost Coast|Lost Coast]] region, Korvosa considers itself the founding seat of culture in an otherwise lawless region. Indeed, the great city offers a safe place and a home for many of the frontier people. Not all is well and good for the Korvosan citizens, however - the city is often plagued by rampant crime, draconian rules kept in place by conservative powers, monster attacks, and conflict with both the city's neighbors and Varisian natives.

Those who live in Korvosa both respect and admire ostentatious displays of wealth, power, or knowledge. They consider confidence and competence the greatest of assets, and they deride or heckle those who display weakness, indecisiveness, or lack of ability. Korvosans are quick to judge and slow to forgive. They like to capitalize words, and many feel this minor trait gives their works and creations an air of greatness and importance. In addition to power, Korvosans love predictability. They like to regulate their lives, creating strict regimens for themselves that they then slavishly follow. Upsetting a Korvosan’s routine can ruin his entire day and is likely to make him cranky and belligerent. To this end, Korvosa militantly enforces its laws (which often have harsh punishments far in excess of those of other nonevil governments) and rewards those who play by the rules.  

A day in Korvosa starts early with the dawn, the sea breeze instilling the smells of salt and brine into the open windows. Dock workers, fishermen and sailors rise early to start their daily works, and the docks and harbor quickly fill with shouts and hubhub. The city markets and stores open shortly after, stalls set under building of crimson red tile roofs, with both nobles and lowly peasants arriving to pursue. Students start their day a little later in the morning, entering the great halls of the Acadamae or the city's other collages, practicing the arcane arts. The [[Imp|imps]] summoned during their training often escape the school, and can sometimes be seen flying in packs above city streets, fighting the local [[Dragonet]] populace while causing a havoc. At noon, the markets flush once more with the lunch rush, as people enjoy today's catches in fish dumplings and fried calamari. At that time larger creatures also appear in the skies above the city - the [[Hippogriff|hippogriffs]] of the Sable Company, their riders ever on the lookout for threats. As evening falls, sailors, students and soldiers all gather in the inns and taverns for dinner and drinks - the people of Korvosa especially appreciate a strong ale to finish off the day. The poorer districts of the city are mostly avoided by both bystanders and guards as night falls, as the chance of one getting their purse - or their neck - cut is quite high in these areas.  

### History
##### Pre-Great Expansion (Unknown - 4507)
Long before the first settler stepped foot in Varisia, the native [[Varisia#Shoanti|Shoanti]] lived in relative peace throughout the southeastern area of the region. They centered their lives around the Grand Mastaba near the mouth of the [[Jeggare River]]; Their oral history spoke of a thing of great power somewhere beneath the sacred pyramid - something they could never again allow to see the light of day. Thus, the Shoanti still consider the Grand Mastaba one of their most important holy sites.  

The Sklar-Quah tribe inhabited the floodplains along the river for centuries, seeing to the mandate of defending the pyramid from [[Goblin]], ogre, and giant raids, or the rare infiltration of adventurers from distant lands. Ruthlessly they have pushed every invader for generations, never encountering a single challenge that truly threatened their warriors. This all changed in 4507 AR.  

##### Fort Korvosa (4507 - 4539)
At that time, an expedition consisting of many settlers and colonizers from Cheliax and many of the smaller kingdoms surrounding Varisia arrived in the area. At their front marched a small army led by Field Marshal Jakthion Korvosa, with orders from the crown to build a new colony on Varisian lands. The Shoanti fought these invaders tooth and nail, but Korvosa's forces were soon bolstered by another expedition, that of the explorer Montlarion Jeggare. A bitter battle ensued between the soldiers and the Shoanti natives, but in the end, the invaders won and drove the Shoanti northeast up into the [[Cinderlands]].

With the natives defeated, the explorers settled in the mouth of the newly named [[Conqueror's Bay]] and founded Fort Korvosa around the site of the Grand Mastaba. For the next 32 years, Fort Korvosa acted as the center of a network of trappers, hunters, anglers, and adventurers who came from neighboring kingdoms to exploit the resources of the untamed lands of Varisia. The fort had its share of trials and setbacks, including regular clashes with the Shoanti, goblins, and on 13 Neth in 4529 AR, a disastrous fire that burned down half the town. In the aftermath, the Sable Company rose like a phoenix to become the potent defender of the city during its reconstruction and into the years to follow.  

In 4532 AR, the great Shoanti chieftain Galstak Sevendeaths (who was resurrected six times) brought together the local Shoanti tribes to form a small army that laid siege to Fort Korvosa for a week, launching 7 years of renewed hostility and war between the two peoples. Finally, in 4539 AR, Sevendeaths was killed for the final time (and thus cementing his name
and legacy in history). The alliance he formed crumbled, and the tribes quickly fell to in-fighting, and were driven once again back into the Cinderlands.  

##### Ascending Korvosa (4539 - 4606)
With this conclusive defeat, Fort Korvosa’s leading families at last felt safe enough to lay claim to the surrounding hinterlands. More flocked to the city, and over the next 12 years, the population of Fort Korvosa nearly doubled. Then, in 4552 AR, strife nearly tore the settlement apart from within during a time known today as the Cousins’ War, when noble turned against noble and the common folk suffered. Eventually, the infighting came to an end with the aid of arbiters from Cheliax, and when the city reaffirmed its allegiance to the Chelish crown, a new era of peace and prosperity arrived. In order to put its past tragedies behind it, the town officially dropped the word “fort” from its name, becoming simply Korvosa. This new era of plenty came to an end in 4606, with the advent of the [[History of Golarion#Age of Lost Omens|Age Of Lost Omens]].  

##### Independent Korvosa (4606 - today)
When the Empire of Cheliax fell into civil war, Korvosa officially became an independent and free city, yet it clung to many of it;s old colonial traditions. This change of power, coupled with the unrest brought by Aroden’s death and unusually powerful storms, racked Korvosa and threatened to hurl the city back into civil war. Yet Korvosa managed to avoid repeating the Cousins’ War, and in the end, two factions remained: a majority of royalists loyal to a crown (regardless of who wore it) and a faction of liberals who wished to see the city thrive as an independent state free from any of the old powers. The liberals eventually left Korvosa en masse to travel west and found the city of [[Magnimar]]. Those who remained behind decided that, with no crown to follow, Korvosa should have its own royalty. Lord Arbust Arabasti paved the way for the ascension of his son, Eodred, who took over leadership of the town upon Arbust’s death in 4624. A fine and popular ruler, Lord Eodred reclaimed abandoned parts of Korvosa,
strengthened its rural holdings, and improved relations with neighboring kingdoms. In 4633, the people finally voted to change their lord’s title to king. A great throne was commissioned
for the Korvosan nobility—a work of art made of iron and clad in the finest of crimson satin and velvet.  

None predicted that this Crimson Throne would eventually come to be associated with a horrible curse: that all who claimed it would bear no more children and die of unnatural causes.

### Geography
![](https://pathfinderwiki.com/w/images/d/d4/Korvosa.jpg)

Korvosa is the southmost city out of the three city-states of [[Varisia|Varisia's]] [[The Lost Coast|Lost Coast]], sitting in a double bend of the [[Jeggare River]] just before it empties into [[Conqueror's Bay]]. The oldest part of the city is located on Endrin Isle, a relatively small spit of land, which is connected via bridges to the mainland across the Narrows of Saint Alika. The wetlands the city is built on are hot and humid for most days of the year, and the coast itself steamy and shallow.  

Korvosa holds a couple dozen holdings surrounding it's borders, an array of towns and villages supporting the city with produce and raw goods. It's northren neighboring port, Magnimar, is both Korvosa's trade partner and economic rival, and Magnimarians disparagingly refer to Korvosa as "Little Cheliax".

### Government
The government of Korvosa is comprised of three official branches, each with its own unique sphere of influence. In addition, Korvosa's noble families exert limited power in the affairs of government.  

##### Monarchy
Since their appointment following the decline of the Chelexian Empire, the monarchs of of Korvosa have served as the primary diplomats and defenders of the city-state. The city's line of kings and queens have served the populace from the famed Crimson Throne since Korvosa gained it's unexpected independence. The monarchy's authority has always been limited, with control of Korvosa's government shared among groups that existed prior to its inception.  

The current moarch of Korvosa is King Eoderd II, an old man who is known on one hand as a skilled leader who manages to negotiate lucrative trade agreements for the city and handle the bickering noble houses, and on the other a scandalous womanizer and gold spender.  

##### Arbiters
The Arbiters of Korvosa act as judges, hearing disputes and crimes and determining punishments for those deemed guilty. They also hold the responsibility of legislative oversight over decisions made by the other branches of Korvosa's government. The most well-known senior arbiter in the city is Zenobia Zenderholm, also known as the "Hanging Judge". She is justifiably feared by the criminal classes for her harsh rulings.  

##### Magistrates
Korvosa's magistrates are in charge of the daily management of the city. The 23 individuals that comprise this body are primarily concerned with matters of bureaucracy, including taxation, economic oversight, and public works. Garrick Tann, the Magistrate of Commerce, is one of the least popular, as one of his duties is to act as the city's chief tax collector. In contrast, Syl Gar, the Magistrate of Expenditures is one of the most popular, as he responsible for spending the taxes on the city's behalf. Mercer Cucuteni is the current Magistrate of Tourism, responsible for making the city seem as interesting and inviting to outsiders as possible. Lolia Perenne is the Magistrate of Regulation. She and her staff spend most of their time searching for the various means by which merchants and customers cheat each other.  

##### Noble Houses
The 36 current noble houses of Korvosa are extended families who control parts of the trade and diplomatic operations of the city. The five most powerful houses are known as the Great Houses, and they enjoy greater than regular benefits by controlling aspects of the city and having the protection of the Sable Company both within and without the city. Each one of the Great Houses has a representative on the council of Peerage Review, advising the monarch on how to administer the city. They have the power to overrule orders given to the Korvosan Guard. The five Great Houses include:
- House Arkona, a merchant family with connections to the far off land of Vudra.
- House Jeggare, the wealthiest family in the city, and with a long rich history connected to one of the original explorers of Varisia.
- House Leroung, directors of the University of Korvosa and custodians of knowladge.
- House Ornelos, the family that heads the Acadamae and produces the most Magistrates.
- House Zenderholm, a family of arbitrators and lawyers.  

The remaining 31 houses are known collectively as the Dock Houses, each a powerful political player of their own. Some of the Dock Houses include:
- House Arabasti, family of the current king.
- House Bromathan, with strong connections to Korvosa's military.
- House Caligaro, importers of religious artifacts and strange idols.
- House Carowyn, with deep dedication to entertainment and patronage of artists. 
- House Endrin, a house of noble warriors who produced most of the Sable Company commanders.
- House Fordyce, once a powerful member of the Great Houses that was greatly reduced in power over the years and is now a shell of it's former glory.
- House Kroft, another traditionally military house.
- House Peltherianon, a house that has degraded to underworld dealings in wake of losing most of it's wealth in economic disasters.
- House Prophyria, a prominent merchant house with a bad reputation due to their cruelty during the Cousin War.  

##### Symbols
The traditional colors of Korvosa (seen in it's flag and in many uniforms of city organizations) are red, black and silver. Korvosa's offical mascot is the [[Hippogriff]]. The city's motto is "Fidelity and Strength".  

### Military
Korvosa's military resources are embodied in three separate organizations, each charged with protecting the city-state and its people in manners unique to each group.  

##### Korvosan Guard
The primary responsibility of the Korvosan Guard is the protection and well being of the city of Korvosa. Its members serve as police and will, in times of danger from beyond the city's walls, also act in the capacity of a defensive military force. In addition, the Guard also often works to forward the interests of the government and the local church of Abadar.  

##### Sable Company
The [[Hippogriff]]-mounted marines of the Sable Company answer directly to Korvosa's monarchy, their orders given to them directly by Castle Korvosa. Their purpose is to patrol and defend the city's skies and waterways, though they have also been known to assist the Korvosan Guard in their efforts throughout the city.  

##### Order of the Nail
The Hellknights of the Order of the Nail are paid allies of the Korvosan government, and pursue their unyielding ideals of order and law from the nearby Citadel Vraid. They are fanatical when confronted by behavior or events that fly against their strict worldview, but support the city only as long as they are compensated.  

##### Vigilantes
As in any city of sizable size, the military fails to defend all citizens at all times from crime, and so some choose to take matters into their own hands. Korvosa has seen it's share of vigilantes through the years, but the most famous of all is Blackjack, a masked warrior who is a champion of the meek and terror of Korvosa's underworld. Blackjack has operated in the city for well over a century now, raising questions whether the dark swashbuckler is a member of a long-living race, a title passed between different people, or something else entirely.  

### Economy
![](https://pathfinderwiki.com/w/images/c/c3/Bridgefront.jpg)

Korvosans see their great city as a cosmopolitan wonder, even though internationally they are seen as somewhat of a provincial backwater. Despite it's remote location and status, Korvosa is nonetheless a major commercial player in the region, and is a gateway for trade throughout Varisia. Its primary export is seafood, such as oysters (the Narrows of St. Alika are famous for their oyster beds), crawdad claws, caviar, and other high-end seafoods. Also in the high-end foodstuff department, Korvosa exports spice made of the Thileu tree bark to Cheliax at ridiculously high prices.  It also exports goods made in it's vassal holdings.  

Thanks to the impressively large Bank of Abadar, Korvosa is an important banking and financial city which mints its own currency. Thanks to the presence of the Acadamae, Korvosa is also a fair-sized supplier of magic items. The Arkona family imports art items from distant Vudra. Finally, as much as the government loathes to admit, Korvosa has an impressive drug trade, although it mainly imports such goods.  

Korvosa's food supplies come from the farms around it and its vassal communities, although it does import foods that can't grow in its climate. Wine is a major import, for example, as are tropical and subtropical fruits. The majority of Korvosa's imports, though, are in "manufactured goods." Despite the presence of the Ironworks in the city, Korvosa's capacity to produce finished goods lags well behind its demand, and like any good colony it imports a lot of such supplies from its homelands.  

##### Guilds
According to Korvosa's charter, no guilds or trade unions may be formed within the city. The only exception to this is the Cerulean Society, Korvosa's thieves' guild. This law was designed to keep groups from price-fixing, but also allows the city to maintain direct control over their labor force. Most craftsmen in the city are self-employed and learn their trade from a master during their youth. Because Korvosa relies heavily on these cottage industries, certain rights and protections are guaranteed to the workers.  

### Religion
Most [[Religions Index#Core deities|deities of the Inner Sea]] are openly worshipped in Korvosa, although the population is not particularly religious.  

##### Main Faiths
Temples exist to [[Abadar]], [[Asmodeus]], [[Pharasma]], [[Sarenrae]] and [[Shelyn]]. Of these, the first four are most popular. he various religions generally keep to themselves, but cooperate with each other when necessary; the inevitable doctrinal differences are resolved non-violently. For instance, the church of Abadar emphasises the punishment of criminals, whilst clerics of Sarenrae have more of an interest in rehabilitation.  

##### Other Faiths
The city's Pantheon of the Many temple contains shrines for most of the non-evil deities of the Inner Sea.  

Aroden was once an important deity in Korvosa, but today his temple in Old Korvosa is crumbling into ruin. Three priests remain to serve the temple, and so far they have been able to gather enough support to block the city's plans to raze the building.  

Druids and other nature worshippers are not very popular in Korvosa, partly due to the poort behavior of a cabal of troublemaking druids who frequent the docks. However, they are welcome to visit the Pantheon of the Many.  

### Magic
![](https://pathfinderwiki.com/w/images/a/a0/Acadamae_students.jpg)

The city of Korvosa has two main schools of magic - the prestigious Acadamae and the smaller Theumanexus College.  

##### The Acadamae
The Acadamae is the larger magic college and one of Korvosa's biggest fame points. Students from all throughout the region flock to the city to learn magic at it's halls. The Acadamae, under cruel headmaster Toff Ornelos, teaches many schools of magic, but specializes in conjuration, and is famous for the many imp familiars students summon as part of their training. Students give ten years of their lives to try and become highly talented wizards. Every year, some junior students die to magical traps, wayward spells or murderous imps - but those who survive and graduate become respected arcanists and are highly sought after throughout the Inner Sea for their talents. One of the Acadamae's greatest traditions is the yearly Breaching Festival, in which students and adventurers try to prove their skill by breaking into the supposedly impregnable Hall of Wards on campus. So far only one student has ever managed to breach the hall, Dycon Fordyce who succeeded where all others failed in 4551 AR, and made his family into a noble house with the fortunes and fame he acquired.  

##### Theumanexus College
The smaller Theumanexus College teaches generalist theories of the arcane. It's students tend to be those who failed the entrance examination to the Acadamae, or those who dropped out. Both the faculty and the students of the Acadamae look down upon the college, but the students of Theumanexus College do their best to ignore the remarks and taunts from their prestigious competitor.  

### Crime
Korvosa has a generally lawful bent, but this has not stopped it from having a thriving underworld. The city's charter actually allows for a theives' guild, and this role is filled by the Cerulean Society. The Society runs a well-organised protection racket. The Society's blue-uniformed employees collect money from city nobles, merchants and temples. Details of those who refuse to pay are given to the Soceity's "authorized" groups of thieves.  

Criminal gangs in Korvosa include:
- The Catsdew Lofties, an all-female gang of cat burglars who prowl the rooftops of the city and prefer to avoid violence. 
- The Dusters, a gang of robbers and kidnappers who take their names from the long overcoats worn by members.
- The Rat's Teat Boys, a gang rumored to consist entirely of Wererats. 

The are also dozens of minor crimelords operating in the city. These include:
- Devargo Barvasi, known to many as the King of Spiders, who runs an illegal gambling house, brothel and drug den.
- Gaedren Lemm, patriarch of the Lemm family, who abducts orphans and urchins and forces them to commit petty crimes to support his operations.

### Inhabitants
##### Humans
A large majority of the city's population are [[Human]], with the vast majority being descendants of the original immigrants and settlers of faraway lands who built Korvosa. Most of these today consider themselves to be Korvosan, feeling no strong kinship to their western cousins.  

Other notable human ethnic groups include the [[Varisia#Varisians|Varisian]] people, who constitute about 10% of the human population, although a much higher percentage of the population can claim some Varisian blood. Pure-blooded Varisians tend to encounter quite a bit of prejudice in Korvosa: most people just assume them to be murderers and thieves. [[Varisia#Shoanti|Shoanti]] make up the smallest minority of humans, mostly due to the years of conflict between the Shoanti and the settlers of the region, and the fact that as a semi-nomadic people, they have little use for cities. If Varisians encounter a bias when dealing with the settler majority, the reactions towards the Shoanti borders on outright hostility.   
##### Dwarves
Due to the city's proximity to the major dwarven state of [[Janderhoff]], Korvosa boasts a sizable population of [[Dwarf|dwarves]]. The majority are merchants and craftsmen, dealing in the creation and selling of tools, arms, and armor.  
##### Elves
[[Elf|Elves]] have existed in Korvosa since it's founding days. Most originally arrived from the nearby forests, although some have made their homes here for centuries. Many elves especially enjoy the scholarly aspects of the city, and achieve high ranks in the faculty of the Acadamae.  
##### Halflings
Though they can always be found in human cities, the [[Halfling|halflings]] of Korvosa are especially appreciated, and known for, their skills as sailors. Although a fraction of the population, almost half of the ships owned by the major merchant houses in the city are staffed by halflings.  
##### Imps
[[Imp|Imps]] are a common sight in the skies above Korvosa, often fighting with their natural enemies, the local Dragonet population. These tiny fiends are summoned by Acadamae students as part of their training, but some nable to fully bind them or otherwise lose control of them. The imps escape into the city where they form into small packs, looking for mischief. They are a particular nuisance in the roof-level Shingles district, and often try to form alliances with gangs or other ne'er-do-wells.  
##### Dragonets
[[Dragonet|Dragonets]] have lived in the area since long before Korvosa was a city. For unknown reasons, they have become mortal enemies of the introduced imp population, and the two species always fight whenever they encounter each other. Two to three times a year, the imps and pseudodragons gather into huge flocks and fight each other en-mass in the skies above the city.

### Locations
The city of Korvosa is separated into six districts: East Shore, The Heights, Midland, North Point, Old Korvosa, and South Shore. Each district is further separated into neighborhoods. The borders between these districts may be fixed (such as by water or a wall), or might exist only in the minds of the city's inhabitants. Two other areas exist above and below Korvosa that might loosely be called districts, making eight in total. The boundaries of these unofficial districts do not conform to any design by the city's planners: they are the rooftop area known as the Shingles and the large undercity of the Vaults.  

#### East Shore
East Shore is the only city district on the far side of the [[Jeggare River]]. It is connected to the rest of the city by High Bridge, and is home to some of Korvosa's noble families with connection to the military. It is also home for the city's second, and arguably inferior, magical school, [[Korvosa#Theumanexus College|Thesumanexus College]]. East Shore is made of a single ward.  

#### The Heights
The Heights sit atop Citadel Hill and the cliffs of Korvosa's western shoreline. It is home to the most powerful nobles in the city, including the monarch, as Castle Korvosa is located within its borders. [[Korvosa#The Acadamae|The Acadamae]], Korvosa's most well-respected magical college, can be found here as well. As the highest district in the city, its residents feel they can look down on the rest of Korvosa, both literally and figuratively.  
##### Citadel Crest
This is the wealthiest ward of Korvosa, being the residence of nobles and rich merchants.
##### Cliffside
A step down in wealth from Citadel Crest is Cliffside, where many lesser nobles and wealthy merchants make their home.
##### University
Named after the University of Korvosa, which can be found here, this ward is dominated by scholastic institutions. 

#### Midland
Midland is what most visitors think of when they speak of Korvosa. Located in the southeast quadrant of the city, it is home to the majority of Korvosa's merchants, shops, and financial institutions. It is cosmopolitan and friendly, the heart of the city. The criminal elements who might prey on this accumulation of wealth are kept in check by the fact that both the headquarters of the [[Korvosa#Korvosan Guard|Korvosan Guard]] and the [[Korvosa#Sable Company|Sable Company]] are located here as well.  
##### High Bridge
The primary inhabitants of this residential district are laborers or the families of those working in the Korvosan Guard or Sable Company.
##### Pillar Hilll
Korvosa's most demographically diverse neighberhood.
##### Slope
This neighborhood features a large number of small and private libraries, museums, and other bastions of culture.
##### West Dock
This rugged neighborhood contains no significant residences, and instead features several warehouses and food processing buildings.  

#### North Point
North Point was the first part of the mainland to be settled after the city of Korvosa began to expand. Encompassing the north-eastern quadrant of the city, it is home to many families descendant from those early settlers, in addition to much of Korvosa's municipal infrastructure. 
##### Five Corners
A crowded residential ward, this neighborhood caters to the city’s politicians and their underlings.
##### Mainshore
Once the site of some of the bloodiest battles in Korvosa’s early history, this area is where Korvosa’s truly old money still resides.
##### Northgate
Many of Korvosa’s non-noble elites and old-money families dwell in this residential neighborhood.
##### Ridgefield
This is the most crowded of North Point’s neighborhoods, plagued with poverty and desperation.  

#### Old Korvosa
Old Korvosa, as its name implies, is the oldest, but also the poorest, most crowded part of the city. It encompasses all of Endrin Isle.
##### Bridgefront
Crowded but narrow, Bridgefront is a dismal and filthy slum.
##### Fort Korvosa
Once the heart of Korvosa’s military strength, this neighborhood is a decaying remnant of its former glory.
##### Garrison Hill
Cramped and bustling with activity, Garrison Hill fights a constant battle to avoid the decay and destitution of its neighboring wards.
##### Old Dock
Only the awful state of Bridgefront’s slums keeps decaying Old Dock from being Korvosa’s most run-down and least-desirable neighborhood.  

#### Southern Korvosa
The newest district of the city, Southren Korvosa was only built in the last decade. Located on the southwestern edge of mainland Korvosa, it does not suffer from the overcrowding that plagues the rest of the city. Because of this, it is a highly sought after residence for the city's new money elite.
##### South Shote
Much more open than most of the rest of the city, South Shore hosts many of Korvosa’s new nobility.
##### Gray
Once considered its own district, the city’s sprawling cemeteries are now considered to be part of Southern Korvosa.  

#### The Shingles
Built on top of the rooftops of Korvosa's most-densely populated areas lie wooden walkways, ramshackle abodes, and shops known as the Shingles. Due to the city's severe overcrowding, this area is home to some of the city's poorest citizens, as well as providing quick highways over the bustling streets for anyone wishing to avoid the crowds.  

#### The Vaults
The Vaults run beneath most of mainland Korvosa and are far more than just sewers, as they incorporate the ruins of two other settlements previously located here.
