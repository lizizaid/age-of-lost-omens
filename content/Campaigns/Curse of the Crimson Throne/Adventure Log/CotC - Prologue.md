Another morning dawns on [[Korvosa]]. The jewel of [[The Lost Coast]], the first bastion of civilization in the untamed wilds of [[Varisia]]. A shining metropolis by some standards, a backwater hamlet by others; But a city made by it's people, nevertheless.  And as it's people awake, wipe the dust of yesterday's troubles from their faces and prepare to meet a brand new day, they perhaps do not know yet how monumental this day is about to be. How, what is to happen tho day, is destined to change course of their entire lives, and to seal the fate of their beloved city forever.  

Ahhh, but there are no more destinies in the [[History of Golarion#Age of Lost Omens|Age of Lost Omens]], are there? No, it will be up to Korvosa's citizens themselves to steer the wheel of time and chart her path toward the future. Specifically, it will be up to a unique group of individuals who, destiny or not, are about to meet later tonight for the first time. They, who form the tapestry of the city's soul, have been chosen - whether by fates read in Harrow cards, or by pure chance - to be the city's wheel-steers. Perhaps, and hopefully, it's saviors.  

But to truly understand this story, to understand where Korvosa is headed, we must first learn the city's history, to go back to her beginnings, before she was a city at all. In actuality, Korvosa's story begins a long time ago, in a place far, far away...  

---

![600x406](https://i.postimg.cc/zXyLWvdh/Castle-scarwall.jpg)

This story begins in a dark place, where the blackened sky is filled with cold mist and storm clouds. Every once in a while, a purplish lightning strikes, the glimpse of light it provides revealing a great, gothic castle with a long bridge across a chasm, it's obsidian rooks piercing the night sky. The castle's guards stand ready on the bridge, ever looking for intruders. When suddenly, with the crash of a booming thunder, here they arrive - a group of eight adventurers, heroes, who have come to challenge the master of the castle. They come from all sorts of lands and cultures, all sorts of walks of life, all united in their goal to defeat the grim warlord who have caused havoc in their homelands for so long. Their leader, a handsome paladin in shining armor, raises his sword forwards, and with a battle cry, the group charges forward to do battle with the guards. The paladin swings his sword, holy light pouring from it to combat the shadows, as his [[Dwarf]] companion chants words of power and summons divine runes to their aid. A [[Halfling]] rogue disappears and re-appears again from dark corners, executing guards one-by-one, and an [[Elf|Elven]] sorceress calls upon the power of her bloodline to create a massive ball of fire - a scream is heard as the explosion knocks some of the guards from the bridge down into the endless abyss. Finally, a barbarian woman, a proud [[Varisia#Shoanti|Shoanti]] warrior, yells as her Earthbreaker hammer breaks the guards bones even through their plate armor.  

The eight companions make their way into the castle halls, fighting through magical defenses and evil guardians, until they finally reach their destination: the throne room. Upon his mighty throne sits the warlord, looking almost like a ridiculous pompous noble with his braided wig and with a glass of wine in his hand. But our heroes know the truth. "It's over, Kazavon!" cries the paladin as he raises his sword towards the man. "We have come to end your tyranny at last! You shall torment our lands no longer!". The warlord, Kazavon, offers nothing but a smirk in return. "Foolish mortals. You amuse me!" he laughs, "You wish to defeat me? You'll find nothing but your deaths here. Do you even know what powers I possess? Do you even know WHAT I AM?". The fearless paladin offers Kazavon a smirk of his own. "We know exactly what you are, Kazavon, and we are prepared. Drop your act and fight." As his wine spills to the floor, and his bones begin to crunch and his muscles swell, Kazavon laughs maniacally. "Very well, then. If that is your wish, you shall fight me. You shall fight all of me, in all of my glory." A deep, purplish light covers the room, and at the place where one stood a man, instead now stands a terrifying wyrm, a great nightmarish dragon of incredible proportions and power. "COME THEN, HEROES! COME AND FACE ME!" growls the dragon as the heroes charge forth. "I SHALL BURN YOUR LANDS TO ASHES AND FEAST ON YOUR PEOPLE'S DELICIOUS FEARS! I AM KAZAVON THE NIGHTMARE! I AM ETERNAL!"  

The battle was long and arduous, but at it's end our heroes stood victorious around the body of the defeated wyrm, proving once and forever that Kazavon is, indeed, not eternal. Their victory did not come without cost, however, as their beloved paladin leader also laid dead on the ground, slain in the heat of battle. The remaining seven did not take much more than a moment to grief their fallen comrade, however. It was clear that death, for beings of such power like Kazavon, was the final stopping point. It was more of an... inconvenience. Already they felt his remains buzzing with power, attempting to reconnect and restitch the foul creature back. Hacking at the bones or pouring acid and fire on them all proved in vain; It was clear Kazavon could not fully be destroyed. But he could be buried away, entombed and forgotten by history. Making a pact then to forever defend his remains from any who would wish to resurrect him, the seven each took a part of Kazavon's skeleton to their far corner of the world, hoping those parts would never again meet. The dwarf took the skull back to his mountain home, where his people built a great stone prison to guard it; The elf took the horns to the forests, and with great magics hid it away from all; The halfling took the wings with her on a great voyage to unknown lands across the sea, and was never heard from again. And the Shoanti warrior, she took the beast's fallen fangs with her back home, to the lands that today would be known as Varisia, to the place where Korvosa would one day be built, where the  [[Jeggare River]] spills into the steaming sea. There, inside an ancient great pyramid where her people have worshiped the spirits since the start of time, they have stored the fangs, swearing to forever guard the evil artifact from any who would come for it. And so they did, standing guard for decades and centuries, standing guard even when their oral history already forgot what they are guarding there. Generations of Shoanti stood guard at the foot of that pyramid, religiously defending the dark secret hidden inside from any harm. That was, until one faithful day, the white devils from the east came.  

--- 

They came from all around the neighboring kingdoms; Pioneers, explorers, settlers wishing to start a brand new life for themselves in this untamed region. They came to the river bay with the pyramid at it's center and found it ideal for a port town, ideal with the exception of the rageful natives who would not leave their ancestral lands in peace. The wars between them were bloody and costly, but eventually the Shoanti were driven away from the pyramid, and the settlers built their town around it. On it's flat top they have built a mighty fort, a castle for their rulers to watch over the people.  

The years have turned to decades and the small port town grew into a large city. And any good city needs a good king. For it's new line of kings and queens, the people of Korvosa have installed a beautiful throne at the height of the castle. However, tragedy seems to befell anyone who sits upon it. Not one of Korvosa's lieges has yet died of old age, all instead falling into disease or madness. And not one of them has yet to have children of their own. It is as if the royal line is bound under a dark curse, the people whisper on the streets. And though not a living soul in Korvosa knows the true nature of the secret that lies under their beloved city, they have given a name for the mysterious curse that haunts their kings and queens, a curse that is very much a part of the city's soul no less then her citizens:  

THE CURSE OF THE CRIMSON THRONE
