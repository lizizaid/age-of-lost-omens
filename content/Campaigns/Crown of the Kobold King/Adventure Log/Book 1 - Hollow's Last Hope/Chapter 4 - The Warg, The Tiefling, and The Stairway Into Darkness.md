Half a day later, tired and hungry, the party arrives back in [[Falcon's Hollow]]. Though [[Buffleguff]] is scared of every shadow in this human town (and indeed, the weary citizens do share suspicious glances with the little [[Kobold|kobold]] sniveling his way in their village), he is dragged into the town's inn, the [[Falcon's Hollow#2. Jak A' Napes|Jak A' Napes]].  

While ordering dinner and some rooms for the night, [[Mori]] notices a young, hooded man with a rapier sitting at the bar, drinking his share of ale while attempting to impress some other patrons with juggling tricks. A fellow adventurer, she quickly gathers - and one their merry band could use, as they desperately need some additional backup to deal with [[Graypelt]]. She sits next to the lad and introduces herself, and he, in return, gives a firm handshake and a smile. "The name is [[Mattis]], a pleasure to make your acquaintance!". Mori quickly explains her offer to him - join up with [[Terror Attack]] to defeat Graypelt and save [[Barlus Chortun|Barlus]]. "Will there be pay?" Mattis asks her. "Of course, I will pay you an equal amount of the rewards we receive for this adventure." she replies. "Well, then, i'm in!" he says, jumping on his feet. "Although I would probably have joined at any rate. If there's a good adventure to be had, and someone in need of help, who am I to refuse? Besides, I feel like this inn is missing a [[Warg|warg's]] head mounted on the wall to truly feel like home...". They seal the deal with another handshake, and Mattis is introduced to the rest of the party. To warm them up, Mattis invites the lot for drinks, and they go to sleep with full belies - Buffleguff especially enjoying his first night on a real bed, cuddling with [[Nachos]].  

On the morn after, the party goes out to get some supplies. Deciding to go cheap on their limited budget, they walk to the [[Falcon's Hollow|Low Market]], where the smells of salted meats and shouts of merchants quickly fill their senses. Though not much quality wares are on offer, they do manage to buy some travel food, as well as some [[Healing Potion|healing potions]]. They also trade with [[Hemet Inkwaddel]], selling some loot in exchange for a good dwarvish dagger ("sharp enough to cut a warg's head off the body", as insisted on by Mattis). With their supplies ready, the party goes out into the woods and once more makes their way to [[Droskar's Crucible]]. During their long walk, before they even manage to get to know each other better, Mori explains to Mattis her crazy plan to defeat Graypelt. "You know, Mattis, though we have not known long, I feel confident you have the acting skills required to be... well, a nice, meaty, bait."  

Arriving at the monastery, the party went into the only unexplored wing of the building, entering a small study that seems like it has been lived in recently. Gnawed animal bones litter the floor, and tufts of fur lie scattered here and there. An old stone desk sits in the center of the chamber, scratched and cracked in many places. The stench of wet fur hangs heavy in the air. A pair of [[Wolf|wolves]] lounge in this room - they jump up when the party arrives, howling and snarling, their maws filled with saliva. Though the party raised their weapons to defend, the wolves did not seem to charge - instead, they circled the entrance to the next room, almost signing to the party to go through with their heads. Carefully, the party went past them and into the other room, where the fur stench is even greater. There, they also find [[Graypelt]] waiting for them. "Ah, the little meatlings return. And what is this? Have you brought me a traveler to consume?" he growls, watching Mattis hungrily. "Yes, O great mighty beast," replies Mori, "he is yours to eat, if you let us pass into the dungeon below, as promised." "But why is he armed?" Graypelt asks suspiciously. "How can I trust him to not skewer my belly as I digest?" "With this little sword? I assure you, it's nothing more than a toothpick for your great fangs, O mighty one..." Mattis attempted to bluff. "Indeed! So you would care not to part from it, so that I may pick my teeth with it after eating you whole!" the warg howled back. Mattis gulped, throwing the rapier on the floor nearby. "Fine, then. A deal is a deal. Go now, and leave me to my meal!" as the rest of the party moved on towards the flight of stairs at the end of the room, [[Lucas, the halfling|Lucas]] gave the terrified Mattis a slight wink, and prepared an arrow. They slowly crept beyond, getting a vantage point - meanwhile, with a massive paw, Graypelt dropped Mattis on the ground. "Now, my meat-puppet, I'm hungry! Get in!" he howled and opened his maw wide, dropping saliva on Mattis's face. But just before he could bite, Lucas and Mori released their attacks on the distracted warg, who screamed in pain. "Treacherous meatlings! I knew you could not be trusted! Fine, I'll eat all of you instead!"  

Mattis quickly takes action, exploiting the distraction. He shoves a quick fist into Graypelt's gut, and with an acrobatic maneuver gets back on his feet, reaching for his sword. However, his way is blocked, as the two wolves run in to help their masters. Mori uses her magic to summon in an [[Eagle|eagle]] from beyond, who circles around Graypelt, relentless plucking at him, while Lucas supports with his magical songs, and Nachos runs in to defend against the wolves. The eagle is quickly destroyed, plucked from the air by warg's teeth and torn apart, while the wolves rip and bite into Mattis and Nachos. "Die now, meatling!" Graypelt screams as he charges towards baffeled Lucas, who manages to duck away in time. With a thunderous spell from Mori and an especially precise crossbow bolt from Mattis, the warg is defeated, falling dead on the floor. A few more swift attacks, and the loyal pack is defeated as well. Though the party is left a bit bruised and bleeding, the way forward is now open.  

Before descending into the darkness below, the party searches the rooms. In his lair, behind a pillar, Graypelt seems to have hidden a few valuables, including a bag of coins, a finely crafted crossbow, a [[Juggernaut Mutagen]], and a [[Magic Wand|wand]] that allows the caster to breathe a cone of fire. In the desk in the previous room, Lucas finds a hidden compartment under the drawers, which contains a [[Weapon Rune, Potency|magical]] handaxe, and a prayer book. The book, written in dwarvish, describes worship practices of [[Droskar]], and a small note on the inside cover reads, "[[Torag]] is no longer worthy of our devotion. Only Droskar can deliver us from the failings of our people." "This could prove valuable if we find a scholar interested in dwarven lore," Mori says, putting the old book in her bag. "For now, let us go on - we must find Barlus soon." With Mattis picking up his rapier, the group follows down the stairs, into the dark dungeons below Droskar's Crucible...
