Making their way through the increasingly dark forest, the party followed [[Mori]] on the path to the lumber camp. The great darkwood trees of the Darkmoon forest, their canopies reaching to the skies, allow very few rays of sunlight to reach the ground. The air, however, is filled with the songs of birds, the chattering of wildlife, and here and there, a small faerie hiding in the boughs. [[Lucas, the halfling]] hummed a well-known travel song as the party soaked in the views.  

After an hour or two of walking, the party stumbled into their first problem: two thin and battered [[Wolf|wolves]] appeared out of the bushes, hunger in their eyes. As the wolves lunged forwards, attempting to corner the party and bite into them, one of them was quickly defeated in a combination of Mori's lightning and [[Nachos]]'s fists. While the second was hurt, Nachos was able to calm the wolf down using impressive natural affinity. He gave the beast a rabbit the party has hunted, and the wolf, still growling, decided to take the offering and run away. "I'm going to come back and tame this wolf, for sure" said Nachos confidently, already deciding on his new supposed pet.  

Traveling a bit further into the woods, the party finally reached a clearing, containing the lumber camp [[Lady Cirthana Gensar|Cirthana]] told them about. A couple of large tents surround a campfire, with numerous wide carts, sleds, and stacks of unprocessed lumber lay amid the sawdust-speckled clearing. Attempting to ask about [[Barlus Chortun|Barlus]] any of the tired lumberjacks working at the camp proved futile - these people ignored the adventurers, insulted and threatened them, or, in a certain case, were too dim-witted to even understand what's asked of them. Mori, deciding it's time for decisive action, managed to find the management tent and barged in, finding the overseer of the camp sitting leg on leg there, carving wood lazily. The surprised man man almost reached for his ax, screaming about his useless men and attempting to send Mori and her friends packing. When pressed for his name, he introduced himself as [[Jarlbren Trookshavits]]. Stubborn as an oak, Jarlbren refused to answer any of Mori's questions, claiming he has better usages for his time then entertain annoying visitors who cause trouble in his camp. "Well, we can always cause even more trouble" Mori threatened. His men will not get a single moment to actually work while their questions remain unanswered, she explained, and they will bother and pester them until someone tells them about Barlus. Her threatening finally broke the tempered logger, who scoffed and spit out he does indeed remember the hunter. He told Mori the man came by a few nights ago and asked for shelter in his camp, but Jarlbren sent him on his way, letting him know in no uncertain term that the only people who are allowed to spend the night in camp had to earn their stay with a dawn-to-dusk session of "bracing outdoor exercise with an axe".  An argument ensued, and Jarlbren suggested Barlus head to camp at "old Ulizmila's place", an abandoned shack of a local legend, the hag Ulizmila. Jarlbren does not know if Barlus took his advice, but suggests the party would kindly leave and go check - and Mori merrily agrees.  

Continuing on their journey for a few hours, the party finally realized they have lost their way in these woods, and that it is getting dark. Setting up camp for the night, they made fire, snacked on some leftover dried meats, and went to sleep. During the night, while Mori watched, she heard a rustle in the nearby bushes. Mori quickly woke the others and got ready, but from the bushes stumbled out a small, confused [[Kobold|kobold]]. He introduced himself as [[Buffleguff]], explaining he is looking for food. Amused by the clumsy creature, the party fed him and asked about where he came from and what's he doing here. Content with his dried meat, Buffleguff explained that he has ran away from his tribe, who were once very powerful and controlled all the lands, but now became weak when the very-mean-and-evil new king has taken over. The king has a funny looking hat that makes him mad, said Buffleguff, and many of his fellow kobolds have already died in the caves attempting to dig out new treasures by his demands. And so Buffleguff, who was not any help at all, was exiled and had to survive without a tribe. He has wandered the woods alone for 23 years (3 weeks in kobold time), hunting small animals to eat. But now that he has stumbled into them, could he perhaps join their tribe? "You big kobolds are weird to Buffleguff, why does only one of you have scales?" he asked, not really understanding Mori's explanation about what a human is. Nevertheless, the party took an instant liking to Buffleguff, allowing him to join them on their travels - on condition that he would be helpful, and guide them to the kobold lair later. Mori asked him if the king has perhaps kidnapped people looking like them, but Buffleguff said he was not sure. At any rate, Buffleguff enjoyed the rest of his meal and a warm place to sleep for the first time in 23 years, and when morning came, he happily followed his new tribesfolk as they continued their search.

![500](https://i.postimg.cc/YqjRXdtj/Ulizmila-s-Hut.png)


Continuing through the forest, the party eventually found themselves in a small clearing. The sounds of the forest became suddenly distant in this glade, and the nearest darkwood trees seem to twist away from it, as if bent by some impossibly strong wind. At the glade's center squats an [[Ulizmilla's Hut|ugly cottage]], with twigs, mud and ivy stacked on it's darkwood walls. From the roof dangle bundles of gnarled roots and old animal bones, like gruesome wind chimes. The trees around the glade have weird, puppet-like fetishes hanged from them. With caution, Mori stepped up the rickety wooden steps to the terrace and nocked on the old door. No one answered. And so, Nachos ran in and kicked the door open.  

Inside, the party found the place in disarray. Old patches of dried herbs and glass containers lay on shelves, wet pieces of scrolls and hide torn on the floor. While the party looked for clues of Barlus's whereabouts, Lucas notes something strange... The old cauldron in the corner seems to be smoking, as if something is cooking in it... But coming closer to investigate proved a daring mistake, as suddenly, the cauldron sprung four metallic legs, opened up it's lid, and lunged to attack!  

![250](https://i.postimg.cc/nz8LfsMb/Ulizmila-s-Cauldorn.png)

The [[Animated Object|animated cauldron]] tried to bite and swallow poor Lucas, who ran away before he could become part of the stew, replaced by Nachos who quickly came to his aid. Trying to fist and kick the cauldron proved problematic, though, and soon Nachos found his arm stuck inside the pot, burning from hot water. Screaming Buffleguff tried his best to aid by throwing a rock at the creature, to no avail. Finally, the cauldron was brought down in a rain of spells and arrows, and Nachos kicked the battered metallic pot away, cursing slightly.  

Looking outside from the witch hut's patio, the party saw an abandoned campsite in the backyard, with a fire that died out not many days ago. They decided to go down to the backyard and rest a little, treating Nachos's wounds. During their rest, Mori sent Buffleguff out to hunt for food, and he unfortunately returned only with a dead [[Sprite|sprite]] he caught from the air. Looking around the campsite for clues, the party found signs of struggle, and small, reptilian footprints dragging something larger off. Unfortunately, it was clear what this meant - the kobolds have got to Barlus. Mori asked Buffleguff if those could have been his people, and he sadly asserted that it may be the case, as the big-evil king does kidnap people to sacrifice for his crown. With no other choice, the party decided to keep going and follow Buffleguff and the footprint trail towards his people's warrens.  

![500](https://i.postimg.cc/pVJThYsp/Forest-Elder.jpg)

Striding towards the mountain range for a few more hours, the party found themselves in the vicinity of another forest glade. This time, however, a witch hut was not waiting for them - but instead a [[Forest Elder|massive darkwood tree]] looming above the forest canopy. The great elder tree gave great awe to the little party members, and they saw many birds, insects, and even [[Sprite|sprites]] flying around it's branches. As they got closer, Mori opened her senses to the nature around them, and felt this tree indeed has a great magical aura - one originated from the fey. "Fey magic abound", she warned the party, "if we encounter any fey creature, do not upset them, do not let them curse you, and whatever you do - do NOT give them your name!"  

Wandering close to the magnificent tree, the party walked around the nearby river stream, enjoying the sprites and pixies hovering and cackling above them. Suddenly, they encountered a funny looking fey creature, a [[Snat|combination between a snail and a cat]]. The bizarre creature looked almost old and wise, with a small white beard growing under it's whiskers, and tiredness in it's eye-stalks. "Well? Who might you be, then? Hulligans, barbarians, disrupting my rest and making a big mess? What is your name?" he asked angrily. "Buffleguff." responded Bufflegudd without a second thought - the collective sigh from the rest of the group could be heard from miles away. "We are merely passing by. We are looking for a friend. Have you seem him?" Mori asked, attempting to persuade the feline snail to help them. The creature, however, continued to ramble on. "Huligans! Barbarians! Your people cut down the trees, and destroy nature! We would never help you! On the contrary, nature will take it's revenge!" at that moment, Lucas spotted something coming out of the bushes near the water. A slithering creature lunged forward, attempting to bite the poor halfling. A [[Tatzlwyrm|tatzlwyrm]]! The party scattered and attempted to batter the creature, with Mori summoning an eagle to join the fray. The creature ignored the eagle and fixated on Mori, biting and spitting poison at her. With great rage, Mori created a cackling lightning bolt that fried the wyrm for good. The party found some coins and a nice ring that the beast had swallowed. The snail-cat, however, seemed content in laughing at the party's misgivings. Though the more hot-headed members of the party wanted to cook the rude creature for dinner, Mori decided to not annoy the fey any further, and in an attempt to appease them, left some jars of honey at the base of the great tree, paying her respects to the forest elder. Gathering up and leaving before being further humiliated, the party marched on to the kobold warrens.
