As the sun sets low in the sky, the party finally arrives at the forest's edge. At the foot of the great [[Droskar's Crag]] they find their objective - [[Droskar's Crucible|an old, ruined dwarven monastery]]. The building is made of simple stone blocks, worn smooth by the passage of time, slowly falling apart. Sections of the slanted shale roof have collapsed, and portions of the outer wall have crumbled. Weeds and wild thorny plants run rampant across the field leading up to the place, leaving only the slightest indication of a path. "This is where my tribe lives now," whispers [[Buffleguff]], "there are many, many traps ahead..."  

Walking into the overgrown yard, the party find a few clues. An old stone well stands at the middle of the yard, with signs that someone has recently got water of it. Along the walls, some ancient stone statues once stood - only one survived intact, a stone dwarf raising a hammer high. Removing some vines along it's base, [[Lucas, the halfling|Lucas]] reads the inscription in dwarven: "All praise..." - although the name of whoever was intended to be praised has been smashed into illegibility long ago. A trails of kobold feet traces lead to the open double doors into the monastery. Entering the place, they find a small dark hall littered with mounds of debris and a year's worth of dead leaves. Opening the door of the small room to the left, the find an old cloak room - now, only a few moth-eaten rags and a single soiled hat hang on the pegs. Between the rags, [[Mori]] finds a single Ironbloom mushroom growing out of the ground - a useful medicinal herb, which she harvests and adds to her pouch.  

[[Nachos]] walks to the next room over, and finds the door locked. With a quick but powerful kick, he knocks the old thing open, revealing a sitting chamber with a chair and a table. Resting atop the table is a half-eaten crow next to a crude knife and a cracked mug. Stepping inside, the lizardman fails to notice a trip rope connected to the door's base - which immediatly releases a net above him, dropping a small anvil straight at his head! With a loud *Thump* and a yelp from Nachos, the party runs over to investigate - when another screech is heard. A [[Gurtlekep|small, black kobold]] runs out of the adjoined room, distressed. The kobold attempts to throw a knife Mori's way, and stabs and slashes with his blade, but does not manage to cause more than blunt damage before being hit hard back by an angry Nachos. Beaten, the creature yelps and attempts a run-for-it towards the other end of the hallway, knifing Mori in the process, but is hit by a lightning arc in the back and falls dead. In the room he came from, the party finds two beds - one filled with bird bones, the other looks like that kobold has slept in it not long ago. On the spare bed, they also find a set of artisan's tools useful for stone masonry and carving. They also find a small sack with some coins, and a single hexagonal and shiny garnet, which Mori feels a faint magical aura radiating from.  

Across from Gurtlekep's room, Nachos moves open a broken double door, revealing a ruined library. The library is in a great state of disrepair, with one corner of the room being completely collapsed and soaked in a wide pool of stagnant rain water. Thick fungus grows on most of the remaining books on the shelves lining the walls. Carefully moving away the mold without releasing any spores, Mori takes out one surviving book from the top shelf - a beautifully illustrated tome, containing Dwarven hymns to [[Torag]]. Folded into the last page of the book is a scroll of *spiritual weapon*, which Mori tucks away.  

![500](https://i.postimg.cc/vZyXJtrs/Torags-anvil.png)

Backtracking into the large room opposite to the monastery's entrance, Nachos once again kicks in the double doors, revealing an old shrine. Darkwood benches lie tipped over and covered in dust on either sides of the room. At the far end sits a large ceremonial anvil, but it's surface is defaced and ruined. "This shrine was once sacred to [[Torag]]", says Mori as they move in to explore. "The dwarves who lived here were probably devoted monks of the Forgefather." As she looks more closely at the ancient anvil, Nachos hisses out in alarm. Two small, black, jellyfish-like creatures who have hidden in the dark corner of the room detach from the ceiling and attack! One of these [[Darkmantle|darkmantles]] sprays an inkly blob of darkness into the room, concealing the party members from one another, while the second descends upon Lucas, attaching to the poor fellow's head and constricting him. Nachos immediately runs through the ink cloud to help Lucas, ripping the creature off his head, and together they kill the monster. Meanwhile, the second darkmantle relentlessly attacks Mori, who in return cannot manage to hit it back with her spells, with Kiki thrashing and meowing about, and Buffleguff not doing a whole lot to help but scream and throw rocks. Badly bruised, Mori let's out a scream of frustration, and summons a powerful thunderstrike that finally rips the monster apart. With the darkmantles gone, the three once again look at the shrine, finding five depression edged into the anvil. Nachos takes out the garnet they found earlier, deducing it is the same shape as the depressions. He puts the stone in one of the holes, when suddenly a wave of good energies was over the room, a sound like a heavenly gong and a divine dwarven choir suddenly hit their ears before fading. The group finds some of their health restored, and their strength empowered by this blessing.  

Satisfied, the party takes a moment to rest in the shrineroom. Mori takes that time to try and confront Buffleguff about his helpfulness in combat. "You cannot keep cowering away every battle, screaming and getting between our legs!" she said, angrily. "We took you in to this party, and in a party, everyone is expected to do their part, and at least *try* to help! If you wish to continue traveling with us, you must do your part as well." looking innocent, the small kobold replied, "Buffleguff does not know what large kobold talks about! Buffleguff is the bravest warrior there is! In fact, Buffleguff saved you from the monsters back there!". This very confident response was of course rebuffed by a very angry Mori, whose rage put the fear of gods into the small kobold. After a good screaming sessions, Buffleguff, huddled into a ball, shakily admitted that, indeed, he was not the "bravest warrior there is", and actually somewhat of a coward. He promised to do his part to support the group from now on, if not in combat then in scouting and hunting.  

Continuing on to the right, the party moved past another dwarven statue and into an old armory. Old cobweb-covered racks and armor stands fill much of what must have been once a well-tended armory, but is now devoid of arms or armor. Though the room looks pillaged, after a minute of searching the debris, Lucas finds two [[Freezing Ammunition]] arrows knocked down on the floor. He also finds the stone wall to the right to be somewhat pressed in. Pushing that secret door in, the party reveals an old prison, made of a corridor of four cramped cells with rusted iron doors. In each cells lies the skeleton of a dwarf. As they investigate the room, three of the skeletons suddenly animate, their eyes glowing with an uncanny yellow color. They rise up, screeching and slashing at the party, but with enough attacks the skeletons are quickly brought down again and crumble to a pile of bones. Searching for magic in the room, Mori finds a magical ring on the finger of the skeleton that did not reanimate - a [[Ring of Torag]], which she puts on her own finger.  

At this point the sun hangs low in the sky, and the party decides to rest instead of pushing on in their search for [[Barlus Chortun|Barlus]] for the night. They go out and make camp in the yard, while Buffleguff makes good on his promise and goes hunting (albeit not very successfully). They choose guard shifts, and go to sleep as the cold night arrives.  

During Mori's shift, as she stares into the fire, she suddenly hears steps approaching slowly from inside the monastery. Quickly waking up her comrades, she sees cruel, red eyes looking at her from the building's door. The creature came out into the moonlight, revealing a large, terrifying wolf-like monster, his maw drooling with hunger. The party gathered for combat, but the creature surprisingly started to speak. "Who are you, little meatlings? Have you come to be eaten?" when asked back who he is, the [[Warg|warg]] introduced himself as [[Graypelt]], the alpha hunter of these grounds. "Did you see a hunter by the name of Barlus here?" Mori asked, "we only seek him, and once he's with us, we'll leave your grounds." Graypelt slightly roared, then replied, "I did see the man you are describing. My kobold allies have brought him here, took him to their tunnels beyond my lair. My deal with them, however, is to eat anyone who dares try and enter their tunnels. Just like yourselves..." he said hungrily.  Recognizing the strength of the creature, and knowing they could probably not handle such a powerful beast right now, Mori took a dangerous gamble. "Would you accept another deal?" she asked, "we would enter the tunnels and return with the hunter, leaving you plenty of kobold bodies to devour." The warg laughed, but did seem to consider her offer. "I wish not to betray my dear allies - besides, kobold meat is stringy and full of scales. Bring me a person to eat - a human like yourself - then we'll talk!" trying not to upset the beast, the party said they'll consider his deal, and got away as Greypelt went back into his lair. After some light arguing on whether or not they should actually find a person to sacrifice to that terrifying warg, the party decided to end their rest early and head back to town, where they could regroup, re-supply, and think about what their next move should be. Whatever they'll do, they now know their target was dragged into the tunnels beneath this monastery, and one way or another, they must get past Greypelt to reach him. For now, they picked up the pace back into the woods, marching back toward [[Falcon's Hollow]] as the sun went up in the sky.
