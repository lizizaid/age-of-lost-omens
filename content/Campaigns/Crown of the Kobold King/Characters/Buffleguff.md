### Info
An adorable but confused kobold, adopted by [[Terror Attack]]  
- **Ancestry:** [[Kobold]]
- **Gender**: Male
- **Class**: Scout
- **Traits**: Cute, confused, cowardly, sometimes violent
- **Relationships**
	- [[Terror Attack]] (companion)
	- [[Truescale Tribe]] (former member)
- **Status** alive

### Description
Buffleguff is small kobold with red scales and large eyes, and from his descriptions is no older than 8. He is dressed in a simple leather tunic, and uses simple weapons such as a small dagger or thrown rocks.

### Biography
Buffleguff was the black sheep of the [[Truescale Tribe]]. From the moment he has hatched from his egg, it was clear he was a no-good pansy. He was too weak and cowardly to fight or hunt, too stupid to build or make traps. He was the tribe's fool, an embarrassment and a liability, and the other kobolds constantly remained him of this. He was harassed, mocked of, and beaten constantly. He didn't know any other place to go to, though. There was not any other tribe. And so poor Buffleguff continued to suffer the mockery of his fellow kobolds, trying his best (but mostly slacking or cowering away from) as a scout and forager.  

This all had gone away when the evil King Merlokrep came to dominate the Truscale kobolds. Anyone who is weak enough to be a liability, anyone who can't break their back in labor for their king, would be sacrificed to his crown. Buffleguff had to run away in shame, become an outcast. Barley surviving in the Darkmoon forest on his own, Buffleguff found himself truly alone for the first time in his life.

### Recent History
Buffleguff met the [[Terror Attack]] adventuring party, who decided to adopt him and take him with them. Not truly understanding the difference between these mercenaries and another kobold tribe, Buffleguff happily agreed.  

Throughout their travels, Buffleguff has started to learn how to deal with life's hardships, such as [[Lucas, the halfling|Lucas's]] death. But he also found a place for himself in the world, a tribe where he was adored and considered a (somewhat) useful member.
